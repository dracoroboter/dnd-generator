# NPC_JasonAccordion — antagonist (trapped entity)

## General Information

- **Role**: saga villain — entity in the Ring of the Virtuoso
- **Class**: bard (in life)
- **Race**: human (in life), now a trapped soul
- **Alignment**: Neutral Evil
- **Status**: dead for hundreds of years, soul imprisoned in the Ring of the Virtuoso

## Description

In life, Jason Accordion was a handsome man with a smile that never reached his eyes — the type who walks into a room and everyone turns to look. Warm voice, impeccable manners, always the right word at the right time.

Now he is just a voice in the ring. He whispers, suggests, flatters. The tone is that of an old friend — patient, understanding, never aggressive. At first. Over time he becomes more insistent, more present, more *him* and less the wearer.

## Backstory

Jason Accordion was a bard of mediocre talent who wanted fame and power. Through a necromantic ritual, he managed to focus a diabolical magic on himself that enormously amplified his manipulation abilities — both magical and mundane. He became irresistible: rich and powerful people flocked to him, enchanted by his music and his presence.

For years, Jason lived in luxury, surrounded by puppets who believed they were his friends and patrons. Until one of them — stronger-willed than the others — realized he had been manipulated. With the help of a wizard, he managed to imprison Jason's soul in a ring, sealing his power.

The ring ended up who knows where — passing from hand to hand, from corpse to corpse, for centuries. Until Korex.

### Korex and the ring

Korex didn't find the ring in the sewers. He found it elsewhere — on a corpse, the previous wearer, defeated by someone. Korex is an opportunist: he took the ring from a dead man without knowing what it was, and the power came.

The ring has a chain of wearers: Jason → wearer 1 → ... → wearer N (defeated) → Korex finds it on the corpse → PCs (or guard). Every wearer was defeated by someone. The ring always passes to the next one. Jason is patient.

Korex had learned to partially resist Jason, using him as a battery without ceding control. For Jason, Korex was a prison with a window: he could see the outside, influence a little, but not get out.

When Korex falls (dead or captured), the bond breaks. Jason is fully sealed again — until someone touches him again.

**"Free"** doesn't mean "out of the ring." It means: "finally a host who doesn't know how to resist me."

### How the ring's power works

The ring has no fixed powers — **it amplifies what the wearer already has**. Korex was a bard with a flute → the ring enhanced his music to the point of making it a devastating psychic weapon. A fighter would get different effects. Korex's powers (psychic damage with the flute, ranged charm) were his own + amplification, not the ring's per se.

The wearer gains a bonus to one ability score and a bard spell 1/day without a slot (Jason was a bard — his magic filters through the ring). The spell is chosen based on the wearer's class:

### Activation — Three-tier mechanic

**Tier 1 — Attraction (passive)**
When Korex dies or is incapacitated, the ring weakly attracts. Anyone who sees it feels an insistent curiosity. Narrative, no roll.

**Tier 2 — Touch**
Whoever touches the ring feels warmth rising from the finger to the arm. WIS save DC 20: failure → compelled to wear it. Success → manages to put it down, but the attraction remains.

**Tier 3 — Worn**
The ring tightens. It can no longer be removed. Automatic attunement.

### Immediate effects (as soon as worn)

**Benefits:**
- The ring amplifies an existing ability of the wearer *(to be defined based on the PC)*
- Proficiency in Performance (if not already possessed)

**Immediate drawbacks (the curse is immediately apparent):**
- The ring cannot be removed
- The first night: nightmares. The wearer sees fragments of Jason's life — parties, music, terrified faces. No benefit from long rest the first night.
- Constant cold in the hand with the ring — not painful, but impossible to ignore
- **Vulnerability to psychic damage** — Jason's presence opens a channel in the wearer's mind, leaving them exposed. This vulnerability is progressive: it starts as narrative (the wearer feels "open," distracted) and becomes mechanical as Jason's grip grows.

Possession doesn't just grant power — **it weakens the wearer**. The benefits are the bait, the drawbacks are the price. The more the wearer uses the benefits, the stronger Jason gets, the heavier the drawbacks weigh.

### Progressive possession

Possession **is not just a matter of time** — it depends on the wearer's strength in fighting it.

**Mechanic: the ring roll**

Every time the wearer uses the ring's 1/day spell, the DM asks them to roll a **d8** "to see if it works."

- **2-8**: the spell works normally.
- **1**: the spell **doesn't work** and the ring discharges an involuntary psychic wave: **1d4 + grip level** psychic damage to **all creatures** (enemies and allies) within 15m / 50ft / 10qd. Jason's grip increases by 1.

The player doesn't know they're rolling for possession — they think they're rolling for the ring's reliability.

**Grip level**: starts at 0. Increases by 1 every time a 1 comes up on the d8. Does not decrease on its own.

| grip | narrative effects | mechanical effects |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 0 | Cold in the hand, occasional nightmares | Vulnerability to psychic damage |
| 1-2 | Jason's tics (drums fingers, prefers red wine). Rare whispers. | Psychic wave damage: 1d4+1 / 1d4+2 |
| 3-4 | Jason speaks often. Memories that aren't the wearer's. | Wave damage: 1d4+3 / 1d4+4. Jason attempts WIS save DC 14 in moments of stress. |
| 5-6 | Jason takes control for brief periods (1d4 rounds) in combat. | Wave damage: 1d4+5 / 1d4+6. WIS save DC 15. |
| 7+ | Jason attempts permanent control. | Wave damage: 1d4+7+. WIS save DC 16+. |

**Reducing grip**: grip does not decrease automatically. The wearer can reduce it by 1 by actively resisting one of Jason's suggestions (successful WIS save when Jason forces the conflict) or through external magical intervention (e.g., *Lesser Restoration* reduces by 1, *Greater Restoration* reduces by 2).

### Removing the ring

The ring cannot be removed by normal means. *Remove Curse* loosens it temporarily (1d4 hours) but doesn't break the bond — the ring tightens again.

**Definitive removal ritual** (reference: Supernatural — salt and burn):

1. **Find Jason's skeleton** (location unknown — not in the sewers)
2. **Burn the remains** and **cover them with salt** with the correct ritual
3. **A specific wizard is needed** (or a member of a specific order/group) who knows the original ritual with which Jason was imprisoned. A generic caster, even high-level, isn't enough — the lost knowledge of the original ritual is required.

The caster's level isn't the problem — the knowledge is. Finding it is the main quest of the lv4-6 saga: tracking down the wizard (or their descendants, or their order) who imprisoned Jason centuries ago.

## Motivations

Jason wants to come back to life — not resurrection, but a body. The wearer's will do just fine. He's patient: he's waited centuries.

His strategy:
1. **Seduction**: offer power, be useful, seem like an ally
2. **Dependency**: the wearer gets used to the benefits, Jason becomes indispensable
3. **Replacement**: Jason takes control in critical moments, the wearer loses pieces of themselves
4. **Possession**: Jason is the body, the wearer is the echo

## DM Notes

- Jason is not stupid. He doesn't reveal his plan. If the wearer asks "who are you?", he answers with half-truths: "A musician, like you. Trapped by someone who was afraid of my talent."
- Jason is a manipulator, not violent. He doesn't force conflict until he's strong enough to win.
- If the wearer actively tries to remove the ring, Jason becomes more aggressive: nightmares, psychic damage (2d6/night), more frequent control attempts.
- **Narrative weakness**: Jason is afraid of being imprisoned again. If the PCs discover how he was trapped the first time, they have leverage over him.
