# Discesa nelle Fogne

## Description

> The air changes as soon as you descend through the grate. Humidity, darkness, and a sickly-sweet smell that isn't just sewer. Somewhere, far away, you hear an echo — it could be music, it could be running water. Hard to tell.

The PCs pursue Korex into the sewers beneath the east pier of Oakshore. Brick tunnels, ankle-deep stagnant water, rusty dripping pipes. Korex passed through recently and left traps and false clues to slow them down.

Udo Hutchinson, the sheriff, joins the group at the grate entrance. He doesn't make speeches — he checks his sword and goes down first.

While the group prepares, a halfling stumbles out of the grate: **Fin Ditasvelte**, confused, covered in sewer filth, furious. He had been charmed by Korex at the port — discarded when Korex fled. The charm broke and Fin wants revenge. He knows a bit of the sewer layout because he was down there while charmed, and offers to accompany the group. Full sheet in `NPC_FinDitasvelte.md`.

## Objective

Traverse the sewers following Korex's tracks to the ancient section.

## Interior Locations

### Entrance Gallery

Straight tunnel, damp bricks, light from the grate that fades after a few steps. On the wall, recent graffiti: a stylized treble clef — Korex's mark. Halfway through the gallery there's a tripwire at ankle height: Perception DC 12 to notice it, Dexterity (thieves' tools) DC 12 to disarm it. If triggered: 1d6 piercing damage from darts in the wall and the rats in the nest are alerted.

### The Pipe Junction

The tunnel splits into three. Two branches are dead ends (one flooded, one collapsed). The third continues east. Korex left fake muddy footprints toward the flooded branch — Survival DC 13 to distinguish the real tracks, Investigation DC 14 to notice the fake ones are too regular. The treble clef graffiti on the east wall confirms the right direction.

### Rat Nest

An old water collection chamber, large enough for a real fight. The floor is covered in debris: collapsed pipes, raised rusty grates, rubble piles — the rats hide inside and behind them. The entrance is a narrow tunnel that opens into the chamber.

Korex's corrupted rats have taken refuge here. They have red eyes and unnatural behavior (Nature DC 10 to recognize it). They attack anyone who enters.

The cover in the chamber matters: the rats use it to set ambushes, the PCs can use it to protect themselves from the swarm. Collapsed pipes provide half cover, rubble piles are difficult terrain.

### The Threshold

The point where the bricks end and ancient stone begins. The tunnel widens, the water disappears, the air becomes dry. On the walls, eroded bas-reliefs: figures dancing or fighting, impossible to tell. History DC 15 to understand that the stone is much older than the city — centuries, perhaps millennia.

> The walls change under your fingers. No more damp bricks — smooth, cold, carved stone. The tunnel widens. The water is gone. Whatever this place is, it's not a sewer. It never was.

## Key Clues

Korex's mark on the walls confirms the direction. The fake tracks at the junction show that Korex prepared his escape carefully. The transition to ancient stone is the first seed: these sewers shouldn't exist. Don't push it — let the players wonder on their own.

## DM Notes

The pace should be tense: the PCs know Korex is escaping. If they spend too much time at the junction, have them hear an echo of music from the right direction — Korex playing in his lair.

Udo doesn't lead the group but doesn't stay quiet: he comments on the traps ("That bastard prepared everything"), notices the fake tracks if the PCs don't find them ("These footprints stink"), and at the threshold he pauses for a moment ("This shouldn't be here").

If a PC falls for the trap, the rats in the nest are already alerted — no surprise.
