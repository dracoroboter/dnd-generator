# Tana di Korex

## Description

> The tunnel opens into a circular chamber, at least six meters high. In the center, a dry cistern turned into an improvised stage: candles, stolen rugs, musical instruments leaning against the walls. And there, sitting on a chair as if he were expecting you, is Korex. The flute at his lips, his fingers moving slowly. He smiles without stopping his playing.
>
> "Ah, my favorite fans. Make yourselves comfortable — the show is about to begin."

Korex's lair is an ancient cistern converted into a hideout. Korex has had time to prepare: he knows the terrain, has placed thugs behind the columns, and has an escape route. Dimensions and layout: see DM map in `maps/TanaDiKorex.md`.

Among the instruments leaning against the walls is a well-crafted lute — stolen loot, not Korex's weapon. The flute is what matters, and Korex has it in hand.

## Objective

Capture or kill Korex. Explore the lair after the encounter.

## Interior Locations

### The Cistern (main arena)

Circular chamber with a raised center (the old cistern) and four columns in a cross pattern. Korex is on the raised area, visible as soon as the PCs enter. Two charmed thugs are hidden behind the east and south columns — Perception DC 14 to notice them before they attack. They reveal themselves on the first round.

The columns provide half cover. The raised area costs 5 ft of extra movement to climb (or Athletics DC 10 to jump up).

### Korex's Alcove

Behind a curtain on the northeast wall: bedroll, scattered notes, a locked chest. Investigation DC 12 to find the useful notes, Arcana DC 14 to decipher Korex's personal code. The chest contains 120 gp in stolen coins and jewelry.

The Anello del Virtuoso is not here — it's on Korex's finger.

### The Hidden Passage

Behind a collapsed column to the southeast, a narrow opening that continues into darkness. Korex discovered it while exploring the ancient sewers and keeps it as an escape route. Perception DC 16 during combat, DC 12 after. The tunnel is unmapped — this is where "season 2" begins.

### Korex's Tactics

Round 1: attempts to charm the most dangerous PC (or Udo — WIS +0, vulnerable). The thugs emerge from the columns. Rounds 2-3: control spells, keeps his distance, sends thugs forward. If below half HP: begs for mercy, offers information, lies. If the PCs don't take the bait, attempts to flee toward the hidden passage.

If captured: cooperates in his own way. Mixes truth and lies. Confirms that "someone higher up" gave him the task, but doesn't know who — sealed letters with an unknown symbol. This is true (Insight DC 15 to recognize it).

The thugs are not evil: if Korex falls, the charm breaks and they surrender confused. If the PCs kill them, Udo points it out.

## Key Clues

Korex's notes reveal he wasn't acting alone — someone gave him the power. The Anello del Virtuoso is on his finger: a blackened silver ring with a musical ouroboros engraving, it emanates a subtle attraction when Korex falls. The hidden passage shows that the sewers continue much deeper.

## Finale

### Korex captured alive

> You drag him out of the sewers, hands tied and mouth gagged — you've learned your lesson. Udo takes him into custody with a nod. "Well done."

The ring is on Korex's finger. Perception DC 10 to notice it (it's conspicuous). It slides off easily — the entity wants to change hosts.

### Korex killed

> The silence in the sewers is almost worse than the music. You bring up the evidence — the notes, the loot, the flute.

The ring is on the corpse's finger. It loosens on its own. The attraction (Tier 1) is active.

### The Cliffhanger — The Anello del Virtuoso

See the ring's operational cheat sheet in `DiscussioneNarrativa.md`.

**If a PC touches the ring**: WIS save DC 20. Failure → compelled to wear it.

> The moment the ring slides onto your finger, the world stops for an instant. A warmth rises from the finger, to the arm, to the chest. Then a laugh — low, deep, satisfied — echoes through the sewers. It doesn't come from the ring. It comes from everywhere.
>
> *"Free. Finally free."*
>
> The ring tightens. It won't come off anymore. And for an instant — just an instant — you see something in your eyes reflected in a puddle of water. Not your face. Someone else's. Smiling.

Mechanical effects: +2 to one ability score (see table by class in `NPC_JasonAccordion.md`), advantage on saves against charm. Curse: progressive possession.

**If no PC touches it**: the ring is handed over to the Guard with the loot.

> You return to the surface, hand over Korex and the loot. Udo pays you. Everything seems over. But as you walk away from the docks, you hear a scream behind you. You turn around: one of the guards is standing still, motionless, with the ring on her finger. Her eyes are different. She smiles — a smile that isn't hers.
>
> *"Free! Finally free!"*
>
> The guard draws her sword.

In this scenario the PCs fight the possessed guard (fighter with +2 STR, *Heroism*). After defeating her, the ring remains — and the problem with it.

## DM Notes

The fight with Korex is the climax. It should feel like a boss fight even though Korex doesn't fight directly — he's a manipulator who charms, runs, and sends others ahead.

The cliffhanger comes after the milestone, when the players feel satisfied. The tonal shift is intentional.

If Korex escapes through the hidden passage, don't pursue him in this adventure — it becomes a hook for season 2.
