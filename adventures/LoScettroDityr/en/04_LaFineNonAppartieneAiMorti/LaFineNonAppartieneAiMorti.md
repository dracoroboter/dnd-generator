# The End Does Not Belong to the Dead

## Prelude — After Svoalbard

> ⚠ *This prelude should be adapted by the DM based on how Module C concluded and the time elapsed.*

After returning the Scepter to the altar of Tyr at Svoalbard (or after the destruction of the Scepter in Module B), the PCs make their way back south. The return journey is long but uneventful — Vecna has lost his main instrument (Othran) and for now retreats into the shadows.

Meanwhile, in Orcastle things have changed. The fall of the magical dome (if the PCs used the scroll in Module A) or the weakening of Malebranche allowed the prisoners to rebel. **Kreig Highlander** — now **Count Wildforge** (he took back the family surname, abandoning the gang's name) — has taken control of the county with **Miranda** and **Ruby** as vassals, old rivalries set aside by the need to rebuild.

**Frankie** and **Ombrascura**, having separated from the PCs after the escape from Orcastle, stayed in the county to help with reconstruction. They witnessed Malebranche's desperation and his ritual to summon Zikle before he fled to Uluvin — that is how they know about the curse.

Weeks pass, perhaps months. The PCs receive the invitation to **Tungsten's** wedding in Waterdeep and set out along the Sword Coast. It is during this journey that Malebranche's curse begins to manifest.

## Description

> The Sword Coast stretches before you, jagged with wind-battered cliffs and dense forests that open onto flowering clearings. The sea breeze mingles with the scent of flowers and bark. The paths are silent, but as the sun sets something changes in the air. Whispers carried by the wind, shadows among the ruins. It is not uncommon to find fresh tracks in the mud — bandits or creatures of the night walk this same road.

Malebranche was defeated and driven from Orcastle, but in desperation he performed one last act of vengeance: a ritual that summoned **Zikle**, the extremely powerful 15th-level wizard who authored the scroll of judgment (the one that had created the dome over Orcastle). Malebranche subjugated him and through him cast a curse on his enemies.

Malebranche has taken refuge in **Uluvin**, a small village in the Delimbiyr Vale, not too far from his county and close to Waterdeep. He lives in a shack outside the village under a false identity. In town they know he arrived only a few months ago — the dates coincide with the flight from Orcastle — and he rarely visits the village. A couple of commoners could testify, not too easily, that near his house they saw him performing rituals. Since then they have been afraid and have not approached again. Only Zikle knows that Malebranche is hiding there.

The characters, victorious, are traveling toward Waterdeep for the wedding of **Tungsten**, who has left the radiant citadel, moved to the Sword Coast, and opened a tavern — ***All'orco che rutta*** — together with **Grimilda**, the bearded dwarf woman he is about to marry. They are coming from the north, a considerable time after the fall of Orcastle and the delivery of the scepter to Tyr.

The curse begins to manifest along the journey: the dead rise wherever the PCs pass, healing is halved, and people begin to fear them.

### Malebranche's Curse

The curse has four effects:

1. Around the PCs, in a 10 km area, every living creature has disadvantage on saving throws against necromantic spells. Undead receive advantage on attack rolls and against Turn Undead.
2. Creatures that die automatically become undead after 1 round from death (1d20: 19–20 ghoul, even skeleton, odd zombie), unless they were killed by a radiant effect or specific spells. If killed directly by the PCs they become **ghouls**. Undead that survive a day acquire this same effect on those they kill: the contagion spreads quickly.
3. Any healing or regeneration effect is halved.
4. Undead receive advantage against Turn Undead.

### Clues for the Main Quest

The clues guiding the PCs toward discovering the curse and its resolution are scattered throughout the module:

- **Unusual zombies** — The undead that awaken do not seem summoned by a traditional necromancer. They bear the same wounds as when they died and some seem almost startled or confused before becoming aggressive. Investigation or Arcana DC 12 to notice this.
- **Local testimonies** — Village inhabitants begin to fear the adventurers. A child says: *"My father says that wherever you go, the dead rise!"*
- **An arcane symbol on the victims** — Examining the bodies of the undead, one can notice a hidden magical mark on the skin or bones (Investigation DC 13), with runes meaning "torment" and "curse" (Arcana DC 17).

### Characters Involved

- **Frankie and Ombrascura** — Escaped from Orcastle, they witnessed Malebranche's ritual. They join the protagonists to help repair the disaster. Ombrascura, a cleric, has discovered that his blessing barely works: undead have advantage against Turn Undead.
- **Kreig Wildforge** — Rose to power after Malebranche's fall, attempting to rebuild the county. Harbors an inevitable fear that evil may return. Cooperates by providing shelter and information. **Miranda** and **Ruby** are his trusted vassals.
- **Zikle** — Died deceived and forced to serve his killer. The wraith responds reluctantly, but is the only one who knows the formula to break the curse. Desires vengeance and redemption.
- **Malebranche** — Thirsting for pure destruction, he no longer seeks power over the realm: he only wants to wipe out anyone who prevented him from achieving his goals.

## Objective

Rid yourselves of Malebranche's curse. If necessary, defeat him once and for all. To do so the PCs must: meet Frankie and Ombrascura, return to Orcastle, summon Zikle from his tomb, recover the Monile d'Oro di Ostegard from the Sunken Sanctuary, and perform the ritual to dissolve the curse.

#### The Oath

> *"I will fight the death that walks upon the earth and I shall be protected."*

The group member who pronounces the oath opens the door. The adventurer now emanates an aura that weakens undead in melee with them, making them easier to fight, but at the same time draws their ire: undead attack them first.

**Oath mechanics:**
- Undead are vulnerable to all attacks from whoever pronounced the oath (duration: 24 hours).
- Undead lose all immunities.
- Undead attack whoever pronounced the oath first.

The door is very heavy: Athletics DC 15 to open it.

> *"I regret nothing. But tell me, didn't you kill me out of convenience? How much justice was in your hands, and how much vengeance?"*

#### The Door of Choices

The PCs must respond to the spirits' accusations and then decide their fate:

1. **Absolve them** — They acknowledge the weight of their actions and grant peace to the spirits. Demonstrates mercy, but means coming to terms with forgiving one's enemies.
2. **Condemn them** — They accept that their choices were just and necessary, declaring that some evils must be eradicated. Demonstrates determination, but could cast a shadow on their soul.
3. **Sacrifice** — An adventurer can choose to take upon themselves part of the curse, becoming a bulwark against necromancy and demonstrating their absolute commitment to the cause.

For each question a designated member of the group must answer. If the answers are all sincere and they accept the consequences of their choices, the door opens and the Monile is accessible. If they lie or refuse to face the trial, Sir Aldric attacks, deeming them unworthy.

To decide if they are lying: either based on what was said (where possible), or by explicitly asking if they are lying — in that case they must pass a Deception DC 18 check.

### The Ritual

If the PCs defeat Malebranche, they can perform the ritual to dissolve the curse. The ritual with the Monile d'Oro di Ostegard requires two separate checks, both of which must succeed: first an Arcana DC 12 check to activate the monile's runes, then a Religion DC 12 check to synchronize them with the sacred energy.

After the ritual Zikle appears:

- If they did not pass the checks, he declares himself disappointed but concentrates and allows one of the characters to reroll with advantage.
- If the ritual succeeds, Zikle declares himself satisfied, states that the necromantic power has been dispelled, and vanishes.
- If the ritual still fails, Zikle (Wraith) attacks the PCs in a fury, saying the curse will continue for months because of them.

Tungsten is thrilled with this idea, which his regular patrons also love, and he decided not to give it up to add an extra kick to the wedding celebration. Triggers on 1–3 on d20.

**The troublemaking uncle** — The bride's uncle is a troublemaker. At some point, drunk, he starts insulting random people just because he feels like throwing punches. Tungsten cries and asks the PCs to resolve the situation "peacefully." They cannot use weapons; if they deal him one hit point of damage he faints.

## DM Notes

The module's pacing is divided into three phases: the journey (discovery of the curse), the investigation (Orcastle, Zikle's tomb, Sanctuary), and the final battle. The first phase must create growing unease — the three travel encounters are easy in themselves, but the transformation of the fallen into undead is the real element of tension.

The PCs should arrive at the final battle with reduced resources. The halved healing from the curse makes every combat more costly than normal. The final battle is rated as DEADLY: Malebranche is a legendary enemy and the presence of summoned undead forces the PCs to balance offense and defense.

The moral trial in the Sanctuary of Ostegard is the narrative heart of the module. There is no "right" answer — all three choices (absolve, condemn, sacrifice) are valid as long as they are sincere. The DM must evaluate the sincerity of the answers based on roleplay or, if necessary, with a Deception DC 18 check.

Waterdeep is described in the source as general context: it is the City of Splendors, cosmopolitan, wealthy, dangerous, governed with an iron fist. Money (the Waterdhavian Dragon) is the true engine of the city. Guilds, nobles, trade consortiums, mercenaries, and criminal groups all have a hidden agenda. Being watched or spied upon is normal. This information is common knowledge for the PCs and requires no checks.

The wedding epilogue is a moment of levity after the module's tension. The three events (brawl, uncle, Grunthor) are optional and serve to close with a smile. Tungsten is a character who genuinely enjoys himself and the tone should reflect this.

If Zikle attacks after a failed ritual, it is an additional combat not accounted for in the balance — the PCs will likely be in trouble. Consider having Zikle flee after a few rounds or using it as a hook for a future adventure.
