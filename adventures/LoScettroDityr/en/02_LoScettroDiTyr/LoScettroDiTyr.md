# Lo Scettro di Tyr

## Prelude — After Orcastle

> ⚠ *This prelude should be adapted by the DM based on the players' choices in Module A. The details below assume the most common path (escape from the Torre di Torth), but there are many variants.*

After escaping Orcastle — through the portal of the Torre di Torth, with the scroll, or by other means — the PCs find themselves on the mainland. If they delivered the scroll to Malebranche, they received the 2500 gp and the collars were removed. If they escaped by other means, the collars were neutralized by the Coppa di Blackwood or the pact with Vecna.

In any case, Vecna has taken notice. Whether the PCs accepted his pact at the Torre di Torth or not, the God of Secrets has been watching them and considers them his instruments. In the nights following the escape, all PCs receive the same dream: Vecna speaking in the darkness, reminding them of the debt (if they accepted the pact) or threatening consequences (if they did not).

The PCs reach Baldur's Gate — the largest and safest city on the Sword Coast — to rest, resupply, and decide their next step. It is here that Vecna's dream becomes an explicit order.

## Description

> A mission has been entrusted to you — not by choice, but by necessity. Vecna, the God of Secrets who holds you in his grip, demands that you recover Lo Scettro di Tyr, an ancient crystal relic said to have been wielded by the god of justice himself. The journey will take you from Baldur's Gate to the ruins of the Temple of Lumina, through frozen mountains and forgotten dangers. But the Scepter is cursed: whoever possesses it without being pure of heart slowly loses their soul, transforming into an undead servant. And the mere fact of having accepted Vecna's mission has already made you impure.

The characters are tasked by Vecna with recovering Lo Scettro di Tyr from the Temple of Lumina, a place lost for millennia in the mountains east of Baldur's Gate. The Scepter is an ancient crystal artifact that grants immense power, but is cursed: whoever uses it without being pure loses their soul and becomes an undead servant. Only the pure can withstand its power.

Vecna wants the Scepter to amplify his dark power and transform anyone who opposes him into undead. The Custodi dell'Equilibrio, an order devoted to Tyr led by Sir Alaric il Giusto, seek the same relic — but Alaric hides personal ambitions of absolute power. The protagonists find themselves potentially on the side of evil, and their choices will determine their fate.

The module covers the entire narrative arc: the departure from Baldur's Gate, the journey through the mountains, the exploration of the Temple of Lumina dungeon, the passage into the ethereal plane of Erythale, and the final confrontation with Alaric.

## Objective

Recover Lo Scettro di Tyr from the Temple of Lumina and decide its fate: deliver it to Vecna, use it against him, destroy it, or return it to Tyr.

### Part 1 — Baldur's Gate

The mission begins in Baldur's Gate. The group meets in a tavern to discuss the dream Vecna sent to everyone (see Prelude). Vecna reminds them of the debt they owe and that his wrath will fall upon them if they do not seek the Scepter — but immeasurable rewards await them if they accomplish the task. He provides a cryptic clue:

> "In the place where the scepter fell and justice was blinded, seek the key that opens the path to the power that is rightfully yours."

The clue refers to the ruins of the Temple of Lumina, the place where the Scepter was lost centuries ago during the Fall of the Temple. It is said the temple is haunted by spirits and guards forgotten secrets.

If the characters choose to ignore Vecna's request, sinister events begin to occur signaling the growing wrath of the God of Secrets. Roll 1d6 every long rest:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | Muscle stiffness — disadvantage on Dexterity checks (temporary, resolves after a long rest) |
| 2 | Initiative −2 (temporary, resolves after a long rest) |
| 3 | One level of exhaustion (cumulative, permanent) |
| 4 | A nightmare with Vecna promising death |
| 5 | Headache — disadvantage on History and Religion checks (temporary, resolves after a long rest) |
| 6 | Fleeting apparition of Vecna cursing them |

#### The Tavern

In Baldur's Gate everyone knows who Tyr is and vaguely knows that an object connected to him was lost in a temple. If questioned directly, the locals suggest going to the temple of Tyr or the library. If the PCs enter a tavern, roll 1d4: on a result of 1, a bard performs a ballad titled "The Fall of Lumina", which tells the story of the glorious temple dedicated to Tyr and its mysterious disappearance. In the central square, a notice announces a lecture given by Master Elrin on the history of lost temples, including Lumina, at the library.

#### The Temple of Tyr in Baldur's Gate

In the temple, depicted like a comic strip, are Tyr holding a scepter and the fall of the temple. Sir Alaric, a member of the Custodi dell'Equilibrio, originally from the Ten Towns and later relocated to Baldur's Gate, can be encountered here or in front of the poster for Master Elrin's lecture.

Alaric is more than willing to describe who he is and what he is doing if he believes the PCs are scholars of religion or ancient matters. If they mention Vecna he becomes grim; if they declare they work for the evil god he becomes hostile and will not help them. He says he is investigating suspicious activities linked to Vecna, who apparently wants to reclaim the Scepter. If they mention Vecna they must make a Deception check (DC 12) or be convincing in their roleplay to demonstrate they are not linked to Vecna.

If they manage to earn Alaric's trust, they could obtain additional clues. Alaric knows that Vecna is searching for the Scepter and that the Custodi are worried he might reach the Temple of Lumina. He plans to go to the temple himself, but under no circumstances does he want to join the PCs. He will arrive at the Temple of Lumina a few hours after them. If the PCs attacked the Custodi at the Valle del Giudizio, Alaric will be alarmed and hostile.

#### The Library of Baldur's Gate

Master Elrin is an elderly scholar of theological topics. If the PCs manage to win him over, he will find the book they seek for them. Otherwise they can find it:

- By staying in the library for more than 4 hours
- By making an Investigation check (DC 19)
- By asking the librarian after getting a library card (free, they don't ask questions about the name given)

> The library has among its books "Chronicles of the Fall of Lumina", a theology book written in Common, which tells the story of the Scepter and its curse. The book contains crucial details about the exact location of the temple — namely a map — about the ancient rituals and the legends regarding Lo Scettro di Tyr.

### Part 2 — The Journey to the Temple of Lumina

The route follows the trade road eastward, into the interior of the continent. The journey is about 10 days on foot. The map found in the library indicates the stages, one day's walk apart from each other. If the PCs travel by cart it takes half the time. A character familiar with life in Baldur's Gate will find one easily. They cannot walk or ride horses for more than 10 hours a day, after which they must stop to rest.

The stages of the journey:

1. **Baldur's Gate** — Plains near the sea.
2. **Village of Greenest** — Plains east of Baldur's Gate. No monsters, they can resupply. The locals know the legend of the Scepter in broad strokes.
3. **Forest of Lost Shadows** — Wooded hills. Two owlbears (MM p. 250) and a harpy (MM p. 20) looking for shiny objects. The owlbears walk through the forest to the right of the PCs heading in the opposite direction (east to west); the harpy is flying above their heads. The owlbears attack only as a reaction; the harpy tries to charm them or attacks only if she sees shiny objects.
4. **Valle del Giudizio** — Mountain pass (see below). From here on it is not possible to continue by cart.
5. **Village of Silenza** — Abandoned mountain village (see below).
6. **Rocca Bianca** — Full mountain (see below).
7. **Monti di Eterna Neve** — 3-day climb (see below).
8. **Piana delle Lacrime** — Plateau (see below).
9. **Ruins of the Temple of Lumina** — Final stage.

#### Valle del Giudizio — The Assault of the Custodi dell'Equilibrio

In a narrow mountain gorge called the Valle del Giudizio, the PCs encounter a patrol of paladins of Tyr led by an officer. The Custodi believe — and they are not entirely wrong — that the PCs are agents of Vecna intent on stealing the Scepter. The paladins block the passage and interrogate the characters about their intentions. With a Deception check (DC 16) they can pass the checkpoint without fighting.

**Encounter Difficulty:** DEADLY (4 veterans CR 3 + 1 priest CR 2 = ~11000 XP adjusted for 4-5 PCs lv9). These are sacred warriors of Tyr, not cultists — they fight with discipline and conviction.

#### Village of Silenza

An abandoned mountain village. If the PCs search, they find ghosts. They have no intention of attacking them. If questioned they say:

> "We were here thousands of years ago, during the battle for the Scepter. Tyr is great, Ormut the purest of us. He saved everyone by carrying the Scepter beyond the edge."

They are two priests of Tyr who died after fleeing the battle; they were on the loyalist side in the civil war that led to the destruction of Lumina. If questioned further, they recount that, having fled to this village months after the battle, they were reached by Vecna's forces who wanted to know where the Scepter was. The battle that followed also destroyed this village. After that they continue to repeat the above.

#### Rocca Bianca — Brother Thaddeus

In a mountain village, Rocca Bianca, Brother Thaddeus can be encountered, a monk and former disciple of Tyr. He still wears the insignia (a stylized severed hand) and maintains the faith. He left the order after discovering the true intentions of Alaric il Giusto.

He can be found in the tavern or the local temple. If he understands and approves of the PCs' intentions, he offers crucial information: he explains what the Scepter is and its relationship with the Temple of Lumina, reveals the hidden ambitions of Alaric il Giusto, and warns about Vecna's true intentions. He emphasizes the danger of the Scepter's curse for those who are not pure of heart. If the characters do not trust him, they can make an Insight check (DC 12) to evaluate Thaddeus's sincerity.

Anyone else they meet at Rocca Bianca warns them of the dangers (cold, climbing, monsters) they will find on the road. They can find, for a price, heavy clothing, ice axes, and whatever is needed for climbing.

#### Monti di Eterna Neve — The Long Climb

> Before you rises an imposing mountain range. This stretch of road will be very difficult and climbing will be necessary.

The climb lasts 3 days. The dangers are: intense cold and fatigue (especially at night), risk of falls, and monsters at the midpoint.

**Cold:** Each character must make a Constitution saving throw every 8 hours to resist the cold. On failure they suffer one level of exhaustion. If the characters prepare with winter clothing, the DCs decrease.

**Falls:** Once per day a random character must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC 13). On failure they slip. The next character must make an Athletics check (DC 14) to grab them. If no one stops them and they are roped together: 2d6 falling damage. If they are not roped together: 1d6 falling damage, then a new attempt to grab hold; on failure another 1d6 damage, and so on up to 4 times. In any case, they remain stuck in a dangerous position and require an Athletics check (DC 12) to climb back up.

#### Piana delle Lacrime — The Threshold

> After three days of travel you reach the Piana delle Lacrime. A vast plateau shrouded in silvery mist, strewn with fragments of columns, broken statues, and remains of ancient buildings. The place exudes an aura of melancholy, as if it bore the weight of past tragedies.

The first one or two characters in line must make a Perception check (DC 13) to spot hidden crevasses along the path. On failure: fall into the crevasse, 3d6 falling damage (9m / 30ft / 6sq) and Athletics check (DC 15) to climb out.

Immediately after, the PCs are attacked by a **Roc** (MM p. 260) that has nested on the plateau and considers the place its territory. The raptor is old and wounded — it has reduced HP compared to standard. It attacks from above, attempting to grab the most vulnerable characters and lift them into the air. It retreats if it drops below 1/4 of its HP.

**Encounter Difficulty:** HARD (4-5 PCs lv9)

**Treasure in the Roc's nest:** 1d4 Potions of Resistance, a Longsword +1, jewels and coins (total value: 5000 gp).

Among the ruins, the characters find an inscription carved on a stone slab half-covered by snow. The runes are in an ancient language, similar to Elvish but with obvious differences. With a History or Religion check (DC 15) they manage to decipher the text. Master Elrin's book gives +5 to the roll.

> "Beyond the edge Ormut carried them, hidden forever, only Tyr and Ormut know the secret. The word of the pure holds the power you seek. Use it wisely."

Ormut was a legendary devotee of Tyr, a guardian tasked with protecting the Scepter after the fall of Lumina. Fearing that the Scepter's power could be corrupted, Ormut used a powerful magic gifted by Tyr to hide it beyond the material plane. The inscription suggests that Ormut created a breach to another plane of existence — the Kingdom of Erythale — and that only those with a pure heart and just intentions can access it.

### Part 3 — The Temple of Lumina (dungeon)

> The plateau stretches for kilometers. In the distance you can make out the remains of an ancient city clearly in ruins. Inexplicably, the cold that has been intense throughout the journey seems to have subsided, as if an ancient magic still had effect on these places.

> Continuing through the ruins, you come upon an enormous semi-intact structure, clearly recognizable as a temple dedicated to Tyr. The columns that once supported a majestic portico lie broken, but everywhere you can see the symbols of the stylized severed hand, the unmistakable emblem of the god of justice.

At the entrance to the temple, the figure of Tyr appears before the PCs and says:

> "You have come this far seeking the Scepter, but none of you is worthy. Your pact with Vecna has corrupted you, staining your soul. Until you prove yourselves pure, the power you seek will lead you only to ruin. The Scepter will never reveal itself to impure hearts, nor will its power be yours without a price."

> "There is a way, but it will be a path of suffering and sacrifice. Only by facing the trials I set before you can you hope to purify your spirits and free yourselves from Vecna's shadow."

> "Remember: pure is the magnanimous one who can forgive the defeated, the one who devotes themselves to me renouncing evil and promising future good, and the one who finally sacrifices something for the good of all."

The dungeon has 12 rooms. The characters must prove their purity to obtain Tyr's blessing and access Erythale.

#### Room 1 — Entrance

A staircase descending downward. Three openings to the east, west, and north. The room is completely empty: cracks on the walls, dust, and ruins.

#### Room 2 — Passage

Completely empty. Cracks on the walls, dust, and ruins.

#### Room 3 — The Colonnade

Columns with depictions of Tyr and inscriptions with theological passages from the god's writings. With an Investigation check (DC 12) the PCs find a depiction of the stone golem from Room 4 with the inscription in ancient Elvish "The Guardian of Lumina" or "The Guardian of Tyr".

#### Room 4 — The Guardian of Lumina (Room of Magnanimity)

An enormous fresco on the west wall depicts the Temple of Tyr with disciples entering and exiting. The images of the disciples are slightly luminous — without needing checks, the characters notice this. If they investigate, they perceive a faint evocation magic emanating from the fresco. They can see the inscription in ancient Elvish "The Guardian of Lumina/Tyr", which is the golem's title and duty.

A stone golem is crouched in the middle of the room. It attacks anyone who enters. The golem is clearly wounded. It was originally a sacred guardian of the temple, but is now pervaded by Vecna's dark influence. Full stat block: see `characters/markdown/MON_GuardianoLumina.md`.

**Encounter Difficulty:** MEDIUM (4-5 PCs lv9) — the golem has only 50 HP of the original 178.

During combat, the characters can notice with Insight (DC 12) that the monster fights desperately, trapped in a fate it cannot control. Manifestations of luminous energy begin to emerge from the fresco: lights that take the form of ethereal human figures, whispering broken phrases. With Perception (DC 10) fragments can be heard: "Help... purify... forgiveness..."

The golem can be freed from corruption with Dispel Magic (DC 14, 4th-level spell) or if rendered unconscious and allowed to reawaken. Once free, the golem is disoriented, asks who they are and where it is, then after a few moments heads toward the temple entrance to resume its service.

Only those who spare or save the golem (a single character) receive Tyr's blessing.

Golem stat block: see Enemies section.

#### Room 5

In the lower left a locked chest, pickable at DC 12. Contains 1d20 gold pieces and a scroll of Dispel Magic. In the upper right a statue of Tyr less destroyed than the others, with the usual theological inscription.

#### Room 6 — Bat Room

Similar to Room 3, but infested by a swarm of bats that attack anyone who enters. If disturbed by a bright light, fire, or if they take at least 4 points of damage, they flee the room.

#### Room 7 — The Guardian Defends the Temple

A fresco entirely similar to the one in Room 4, but in which the guardian fights enemies who are attacking the temple.

#### Room 8 — Room of Blessing

There is a well and other symbols of Tyr. A fresco shows disciples immersing themselves in the font at the center of the room. If the PCs touch the water, Tyr appears and asks two questions:

> "Who is evil?"

The PCs can answer "Vecna" or something appropriate.

> "Do you promise to defend the good? Promise three times!"

They must answer "I promise, I promise, I promise." If they do not, Tyr insists.

Tyr's blessing falls upon the PCs if they curse the name of Vecna and promise to save three innocents while bathing in the font. A maximum of two adventurers can perform the rite. (DM Note: potential hook for the next adventure — the three good deeds promised.)

#### Room 9 — The Just Shadows

At the center of the room is a light source illuminating a stone table surrounded by empty chairs, arranged as if for a meeting. On the walls, shadows move, even though no figures are present.

When the characters approach the table, the shadows solidify taking the form of humanoid figures dressed as judges and priests of Tyr. The figures seem to judge the characters with their empty eyes, but do not speak. On the surface of the table, verses are carved:

> "Only the pure can walk in the light,
> but even the shadow of justice can be just.
> Only when you understand both,
> shall the path be revealed."

If Ormut's voice is with the PCs (they have already received the blessing), a Medallion of Justice appears on the table — a magic item that grants advantage on saving throws against fear or necrotic spells for 24 hours.

If instead they attempt to attack the shadows, they transform into 6 specters that attack screaming "Heretics!" and "Impure ones, begone!"

**Encounter Difficulty:** HARD (3 specters CR 1 + 2 wraiths CR 5 = ~8400 XP adjusted for 4-5 PCs lv9). Only if the PCs attack the shadows.

#### Room 10

No treasure or enemies. A fresco depicts the stone giant defending the temple and the Scepter bearer from enemies.

#### Room 12 — Room of Sacrifice (passage to Erythale)

At the end of a staircase that terminates against a wall, there is the passage to Erythale. A magical flame burns in the middle of the room. On the wall at the end of the staircase there is an inscription: "A' lammen en' iand" — meaning "Beyond the threshold" in Elvish (A' = "toward/beyond", Lammen = "threshold/door", En' = "of/the", Iand = "boundary/beyond").

Ormut's voice guides the PCs toward the staircase — but only if they have already received Tyr's blessing (Room 4, 8, or through the sacrifice in the flame below). If they do not have the blessing, they must first perform the sacrifice in the flame to obtain Ormut's voice and be able to cross the threshold.

Ormut explains that to pass through the wall at the end of the staircase they will need to have faith in Tyr: they must run through it, without being afraid of crashing. If investigated, the wall appears solid; they pass only if they enter at a run. An abjuration and illusion magical aura covers the wall.

If the PCs destroy in the flame any object that has real value to them (magic items, mementos of loved ones, a large amount of gold coins), they receive Tyr's blessing. The DM must judge whether they are losing something of real value to the character. Everyone can perform this rite.

If they are not blessed by Tyr they cannot cross the threshold. They can detect the opening with Detect Magic (abjuration).

Those who receive Tyr's blessing obtain the Blessing of Justice:
- Advantage on saving throws against fear or necrotic effects for 24 hours.
- An invisible voice (which will reveal itself to be Ormut, if asked) guides them toward Erythale.

### Part 4 — Erythale, Beyond the Threshold

> Beyond the threshold opens Erythale, an ethereal realm of light and crystal. The sky shines like a perpetual dawn and the transparent ground reflects every thought and emotion of the characters.

The Luminous Guardians are beings 3m / 10ft / 2sq tall made of pure light with translucent wings, similar to angels. They serve exclusively Tyr and have an innate sense of purity of soul. They communicate telepathically. They defend the Scepter because it represents the greatest symbol of justice and order in the multiverse. Anyone who is not pure, according to Tyr's criteria, is seen as a threat.

The Guardians are friendly and take the PCs to the Crystal Sanctuary where the Scepter is kept. They will hand over the Scepter after being convinced of the PCs' good intentions and the necessity of doing so. No particular mechanics: they just need to be minimally convincing and get help from Ormut.

The Guardians explain what the PCs probably already know: the city of Lumina fell due to the betrayal of one of its priests, corrupted by Vecna's promises, who — like Alaric — had promised peace on earth through the Scepter's power and his command. Tyr himself commanded Ormut to hide the Scepter to prevent its misuse.

The PCs will have to decide who will be the Scepter's bearer. The bearer must swear on their life not to surrender it to others, to defend it, and to follow Tyr's instructions regarding its fate. They are warned that it is a great honor but also a great burden.

**Bearer's Curse:** If the bearer violates the pact, after unspeakable pain they fall unconscious and must make death saving throws with no possibility of being healed. If they survive, they will permanently have disadvantage on attack rolls. Only Tyr can remove the curse.

#### Lo Scettro di Tyr — Magical Properties

A divine weapon that channels justice and purity, cursed for those who are unworthy.

- **Sacred Resurrection** — The Scepter can bring back to life a recently deceased creature, provided its spirit is willing to return and the body is not completely destroyed. Functions as the Resurrection spell. Can be used only once. The Scepter judges the purity of the soul: a corrupted soul cannot be brought back to life.
- **Empowerment of Justice** — The bearer gains a sacred aura. All allies within 30ft / 9m / 6sq receive: +2 to attack rolls, resistance to necrotic and psychic damage, advantage on saving throws against fear effects.
- **Shield of Purity** — Activatable as a bonus action. Creates a force field around the bearer and two allies within 30ft / 9m / 6sq. Reduces all damage taken by half for 3 turns. Once per day.
- **Sentence of Tyr** — The bearer can invoke divine judgment on an enemy creature, forcing it to reveal its true intentions. The creature must make a Charisma saving throw (DC 18). On failure, it is paralyzed for 1 minute, unable to lie or hide its true nature. Once per day.

### Part 5 — The Return to the Temple: The Final Battle

Exiting Room 1 by taking the staircase upward, the PCs find arrayed before them 15 veterans and Alaric il Giusto. Alaric demands they hand over the Scepter. The avatars of Tyr and Vecna appear silently, watching without intervening in the battle.

> Vecna says: "Remember your promise." And adds: "Give the Scepter to Alaric."

*DM Note: Vecna is lying. He does not want Alaric to keep the Scepter — he knows Alaric is corruptible and that the Scepter will consume him, making him a puppet. Vecna intends to recover the Scepter from Alaric at a later time, when he will be too weak to resist. If the PCs give it to him, Vecna wins in the long term.*

> Tyr says: "Remember your promise. Use it for good, or destroy it."

The PCs can fight. If they manage to make Alaric confess his dark plans for power (using the Sentence of Tyr or through roleplay), the veterans turn against him.

If the golem from Room 4 was not defeated earlier, it stands beside the PCs at the entrance and fights on their side.

## Key Clues

- **Vecna's clue** in the tavern points to the Temple of Lumina.
- **The book "Chronicles of the Fall of Lumina"** in the library of Baldur's Gate contains the temple map and the story of the Scepter.
- **Sir Alaric** at the temple of Tyr reveals that Vecna seeks the Scepter and that the Custodi want to prevent it.
- **The ghosts of Silenza** speak of Ormut who carried the Scepter "beyond the edge".
- **Brother Thaddeus** at Rocca Bianca reveals Alaric's true ambitions and warns about the curse.
- **The inscription at the Piana delle Lacrime** confirms that Ormut hid the Scepter in another plane and that "the word of the pure" is the key.
- **Tyr's words** at the temple indicate the three paths of purity: magnanimity (forgiving the golem), devotion (the rite at the font), sacrifice (the flame in Room 12).

## Finale

With the Scepter in hand, the characters must decide its fate:

1. **Deliver the Scepter to Alaric** — Vecna will no longer persecute them, but the bearer risks dying from the curse of the violated pact, and Tyr will become their enemy, slowly taking their soul.
2. **Use the Scepter to fight Vecna** — They must first defeat Alaric in the final battle. Vecna will be their mortal enemy forever.
3. **Destroy the Scepter** — The bearer can destroy it by finding a magical way to inflict 100 damage in a single blow. This information can be requested from the Luminous Guardians or from Tyr. This is one of the three good deeds required by Tyr to become pure.
4. **Return the Scepter to Tyr** — Hook for a future adventure. This is also one of the three good deeds.

**Narrative pacing:** The module alternates phases of exploration and roleplay (Baldur's Gate, Silenza, Rocca Bianca, Erythale) with phases of environmental challenge (the climb) and combat (Valle del Giudizio, Yeti, Roc, dungeon, final battle). The temple dungeon is more a path of moral trials than a traditional dungeon — the combats are avoidable or solvable through roleplay.

**Alaric and the veterans:** The final battle is classified DEADLY, but it is designed to be resolved even without direct combat. If the PCs use the Sentence of Tyr or convince Alaric to reveal his true intentions, the veterans revolt. The DM should make this possibility clear through the clues from Brother Thaddeus and the Luminous Guardians.

**Plan B — if roleplay fails:** If the PCs cannot make Alaric confess and combat begins, the veterans are not fanatics — they are loyal soldiers but not suicidal. When Alaric drops below half HP (45), his Aura of Corruption wavers and the nearest veterans (1d6) hesitate and stop fighting for 1 round. If the PCs seize the opportunity to accuse Alaric aloud (Persuasion DC 14, with advantage if they have evidence from Thaddeus), the hesitating veterans revolt. The rest follow within 2 rounds. Without this intervention, the combat is an almost certain TPK.

**The golem:** If the PCs spare it in Room 4, the golem fights on their side in the final battle. It is a powerful but fragile ally (50 HP). If they defeated it, it is not present in the final battle.

**The blessings:** There are three ways to obtain Tyr's blessing: saving the golem (one PC only), the rite at the font in Room 8 (maximum two PCs), the sacrifice in the flame in Room 12 (everyone). Without at least one blessing, the PCs cannot cross the threshold to Erythale.

**Vecna and punishment:** If the PCs ignore the mission, the d6 table of sinister effects makes life progressively harder. The exhaustion level on result 3 is cumulative and permanent — use it sparingly.

**Future hooks:** The three good deeds promised to Tyr during the rite in Room 8 are an explicit hook for subsequent adventures. The destruction or return of the Scepter counts as one of these deeds.

### The Lore

**The Fall of the Temple of Lumina:** Lo Scettro di Tyr was lost during the Fall of the Temple of Lumina, a catastrophic event that occurred thousands of years ago. During an attack by a faction of priests suspected of being under Vecna's influence, the main temple dedicated to Tyr was besieged. In the chaos of battle, a young priest corrupted by Vecna's promises of power stole the Scepter and attempted to deliver it to Vecna. However, the traitor was stopped by Ormut, a spellcaster who teleported the artifact to an unknown location before dying at the hands of the traitors. Since then, the Scepter has remained hidden, its location lost in time.

**The Custodi dell'Equilibrio:** An order dedicated to maintaining the balance between good and evil in the world, embodying the principles of justice and impartiality promoted by Tyr. Historically tasked with protecting Lo Scettro di Tyr, the supreme symbol of divine justice.

**Alaric's secrets:** Behind his facade of righteousness, Alaric hides a desire for absolute power. He wants to use the Scepter not only to restore peace, but to establish a world order under his control. He has discovered ancient rituals that would allow him to harness the Scepter's power to ascend to a semi-divine state, replacing Tyr and reshaping the world according to his vision.

**Vecna:** He is not pure, so he cannot recover the Scepter himself — he discovered this over the years and therefore must send someone else.
