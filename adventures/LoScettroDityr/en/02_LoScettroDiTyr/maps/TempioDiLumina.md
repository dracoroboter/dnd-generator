# Temple of Lumina — DM Map

## Overview

Ancient temple dedicated to Tyr, located at the end of the Piana delle Lacrime, on a mountain plateau east of Baldur's Gate (approximately 10 days of travel). The temple is semi-intact: broken columns, collapsed portico, but everywhere the symbols of Tyr's stylized severed hand can be seen. The intense cold of the journey inexplicably subsides near the temple, as if an ancient magic still had effect.

The dungeon has 12 rooms arranged across multiple levels (descending stairs). The PCs must prove their purity to obtain Tyr's blessing and access the ethereal plane of Erythale.

At the temple entrance, Tyr appears and warns the PCs that the pact with Vecna has corrupted them and that they must purify themselves.

### Room 1 — Entrance

- **Description:** Staircase descending downward. Three openings to the east, west, and north.
- **Contents:** Completely empty. Cracks on the walls, dust and ruins.
- **Exits:** East (→ Room 5), West (→ Room 6), North (→ Room 2).

### Room 2 — Passage

- **Description:** Connecting room.
- **Contents:** Completely empty. Cracks on the walls, dust and ruins.
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 1), North (→ Room 3).

### Room 3 — The Colonnade

- **Description:** Columns with depictions of Tyr and inscriptions with theological passages.
- **Contents:** Investigation DC 12 to find a depiction of the golem from Room 4 with an inscription in ancient Elvish: "The Guardian of Lumina" / "The Guardian of Tyr."
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 2), North (→ Room 4).

### Room 4 — The Guardian of Lumina (Room of Magnanimity)

- **Description:** Enormous fresco on the west wall: Temple of Tyr with luminous disciples. Inscription in ancient Elvish: "The Guardian of Lumina/Tyr."
- **Contents:** **Stone Golem** crouched in the center. Attacks anyone who enters. Has only 50 HP of the original 178 (wounded). Pervaded by Vecna's dark influence.
- **Hazards:** Combat with the golem (MEDIUM for 4-5 PCs lv9).
- **Secret:** Insight DC 12 to notice it fights desperately. Perception DC 10 to hear the figures in the fresco whispering "Help... purify... forgiveness..." Can be freed with Dispel Magic DC 14 (4th level) or knocked unconscious and left to reawaken. Whoever spares/saves it receives **Tyr's blessing** (one PC only).
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 3), North (→ Room 7).

### Room 5 — Chest

- **Description:** Side room accessible from the east of Room 1.
- **Contents:** Locked chest in the lower left (Thieves' Tools DC 12). Contains: 1d20 gold coins + scroll of Dispel Magic. Statue of Tyr in the upper right with a theological inscription.
- **Exits:** West (→ Room 1).

### Room 6 — Bat Room

- **Description:** Similar to Room 3 (columns and inscriptions).
- **Contents:** Swarm of bats that attack anyone who enters. They flee if exposed to bright light, fire, or if they take at least 4 HP of damage.
- **Exits:** East (→ Room 1).

### Room 7 — The Guardian Defends the Temple

- **Description:** Fresco similar to Room 4, but depicting the guardian fighting enemies attacking the temple.
- **Contents:** No enemies or treasure. Purely narrative element.
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 4), North (→ Room 8).

### Room 8 — Room of the Blessing

- **Description:** Well/fountain at the center and symbols of Tyr. Fresco of disciples immersing themselves in the fountain.
- **Contents:** If the PCs touch the water, Tyr appears and asks two questions.
- **Secret:** Tyr asks "Who is evil?" (answer: "Vecna" or equivalent) and "Do you promise to defend the good? Promise three times!" (answer: "I promise, I promise, I promise"). A maximum of **two** adventurers can perform the rite. Whoever curses Vecna and promises to save three innocents while bathing in the fountain receives **Tyr's blessing**. The three promised good deeds are hooks for future adventures.
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 7), North (→ Room 9).

### Room 9 — The Righteous Shadows

- **Description:** Light source at the center illuminating a stone table surrounded by empty chairs. Shadows move on the walls.
- **Contents:** The shadows solidify into figures of judges and priests of Tyr. They do not speak. Verses carved on the table: "Only the pure can walk in the light, / but even the shadow of justice can be just. / Only when you understand both, / shall the path be revealed."
- **Secret:** If the voice of Ormut is with the PCs (they already have the blessing), a **Medaglione della Giustizia** appears on the table (advantage on saving throws against fear/necrotic for 24h). If they attack the shadows: 2 Wraith (CR 5, HP 67) + 3 Spectre (CR 1, HP 22) — HARD.
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 8), North (→ Room 10).

### Room 10 — Fresco of the Giant

- **Description:** Fresco of the stone giant defending the temple and the bearer of the Scepter from enemies.
- **Contents:** No treasure or enemies. Narrative element.
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 9), North (→ Room 12).

### Room 12 — Room of Sacrifice (passage to Erythale)

- **Description:** Staircase ending against a wall. Magical flame at the center of the room.
- **Contents:** Inscription on the wall: "A' lammen en' iand" — "Beyond the threshold" in Elvish (A' = toward/beyond, Lammen = threshold/door, En' = of/the, Iand = boundary/beyond).
- **Secret — The flame:** Destroying an object of real value to the character in the flame (magic items, keepsakes, many gold coins) grants **Tyr's blessing**. All PCs can perform this rite. The DM judges whether the sacrifice is genuine.
- **Secret — The wall:** The voice of Ormut guides the PCs (only if they have the blessing). Explains they must run through the wall with faith, without fear of crashing. The wall appears solid if investigated. Aura of abjuration and illusion. Detect Magic reveals the opening. Without the blessing, they cannot pass through.
- **Exits:** South (→ Room 10). Through the wall → **Erythale** (ethereal plane).

## DM Notes

### Tyr's Blessings — Three Paths

There are three ways to obtain the blessing, required to access Erythale:

1. **Magnanimity (Room 4)** — Spare/save the golem. One PC only.
2. **Devotion (Room 8)** — Rite at the fountain with Tyr. Maximum two PCs.
3. **Sacrifice (Room 12)** — Burn a valuable object in the flame. All PCs.

Without at least one blessing in the group, the PCs cannot cross the threshold to Erythale.

### The Golem After Room 4

If spared, the golem heads to the temple entrance to resume its service. In the **final battle** (Part 5, exiting the temple), it fights alongside the PCs against Alaric and the veterans. It has 50 HP — a powerful but fragile ally.

### Final Battle at the Exit

Exiting Room 1 by taking the ascending staircase, the PCs find 15 veterans + Alaric il Giusto arrayed before them. The avatars of Tyr and Vecna observe without intervening. If the PCs use the Sentenza di Tyr or make Alaric confess, the veterans turn against him.

### Room 11

Room 11 is not described in the module. The path goes from Room 10 directly to Room 12.
