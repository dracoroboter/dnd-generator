# Escape from Orcastle

## Description

> The air reeks of salt and desperation. Below you, a fortress-island wrapped in a semi-transparent magical dome stands against the grey sea. Orcastle — once a center of power and trade, now an open-air prison where criminals, rebels, and dissidents are locked away forever. No one enters, no one leaves, except through the controlled flights of Malebranche's flying ships.

The city-state of Orcastle has been transformed into an open-air prison, surrounded by an impenetrable magical barrier. The corrupt governor Lord Cedric Malebranche has built his power by exploiting this cruel system, imprisoning political enemies and dealing in the shadows with the worst criminals of the region.

Malebranche has lost an ancient artifact, the Pergamena del Giudizio, which contains information capable of overturning his rule. The scroll ended up in the hands of Malachias Ombrascura, a dragonborn cleric hiding among the ruins of Orcastle. Malebranche has kidnapped the PCs and forced them, via explosive collars, to recover the scroll for him. If they fail, they die. If they succeed, they will receive 2500 gold pieces — but Ombrascura has his own plans.

The island is divided into four zones controlled by different factions: East (Ruby gang), West (Emerald gang), North (Torre di Torth, infested with monsters), and Center (Castro degli Highlander). The PCs will need to navigate between these factions, discover where the scroll is hidden, and find a way to escape the island.

Sandbox adventure for 5-6 PCs of level 8.

## Objective

**Stated:** Recover the Pergamena del Giudizio on behalf of Malebranche and deliver it in exchange for the removal of the explosive collars and 2500 gold pieces.

**Actual:** The PCs will have to decide who to deliver the scroll to — Malebranche, Ombrascura, or use it for their own purposes — and find a way to escape Orcastle before the collars explode (seventh long rest).

## Internal Locations

### The Kidnapping and the Flying Ship Airborne

The dwarf Kurzum, owner of the Giavellotto Fantasma (weapons shop), offers the PCs a reward of 2500 gp for a blind mission. The only detail: "you won't like it."

At the end of another adventure, or during a moment of outdoor rest, anyone with a passive Perception of at least 15 sees a shadow against the sun. A Perception check at DC 12 reveals a flying object approaching rapidly.

A powerful sleep spell hits the group. The character with the most hit points manages to see, before collapsing, the flying machine Airborne landing and a garrison of dozens of soldiers surrounding the group.

> You awaken in a narrow corridor, resembling the interior of an airship. Side cabins open along the walls, and at the far end a larger room — the command bridge. You no longer have weapons, armor, or equipment. Around your neck you feel an annoying metal collar that you don't remember putting on.

The dwarf **Mesusu Merconè**, captain of the ship, invites them to sit. Five armed guards are present (stats: veterans). Mesusu explains:

- The collars will kill them if they disobey or leave the fortress before finding the scroll
- They agreed to the mission on behalf of Malebranche, brokered by Kurzum
- They must find the Pergamena del Giudizio, stolen by Ombrascura
- No one will come to pick them up: it's too dangerous. The ships patrol the skies and parachute supplies once a month onto the outer peninsula to the southeast. They only rarely land for guard rotation
- The collars will explode anyway after one week (on the seventh long rest)
- When they deliver the scroll to Malebranche, the collar will be removed and 2500 gp delivered. Malebranche is lawful evil but has no intention of betraying this pact

Mesusu does not reveal, even if questioned, that she can locate the PCs on the island thanks to the collars.

The "specialist" Frankie Partenope is then introduced (see `../../characters/markdown/NPC_FrankiePartenope.md`): a criminal who has already managed to escape from Orcastle. Frankie does not answer any questions before arriving on the island. Mesusu, if questioned, adds only that there is a door in the dome upon arrival and that Ombrascura is a dragonborn.

### A Traumatic Beginning — The Fall onto the Island

After a long rest, the PCs are invited to the stern and lined up along the cargo door. Suddenly, without warning, the door opens and they are thrown out of the aircraft while still in flight.

> Mesusu, as you plummet, shouts: "Run through the door, it won't stay open for long!"

They fall from 200m / 660ft / 132sq. Every 50m / 165ft / 33sq, ask the PCs if they want to do something. At 50m / 165ft / 33sq altitude they are enveloped by a colored mist: a powerful spell slows the fall. They reach the ground as if falling from 6m / 20ft / 4sq, taking 2d6 damage. With an Acrobatics check at DC 12 they halve the damage.

Before them opens a magical door — a semi-transparent distortion in the dome surrounding the fortress. A squad of 2d4 bandits dressed poorly in red (malnourished, poorly equipped) is approaching quickly: they are 150m / 500ft / 100sq from the door, the PCs are 50m / 165ft / 33sq away. The bandits will take two or three turns to arrive. From above, the Airborne and Malebranche's soldiers exchange crossbow and bow fire with the bandits.

If the bandit squad manages to exit the dome, they are hit from above by the ship's weapons and turn back.

The squad approaches asking who they are and where they come from. With Persuasion or Deception at DC 10, the PCs can convince them they are not a threat and are taken to Axel Ruby. If they fight, when the first bandit reaches half HP they surrender or flee. A bandit captain arrives to parley; three more bandit captains arrive after two turns.

### East — The Ruby County

The eastern zone of the island is governed by Axel Ruby, a former vassal of Malebranche imprisoned through a power play. Everyone wears red insignia. Axel hates Miranda Emerald and blames her for his fate.

The PCs meet Axel with a garrison of 2d4 bandits in good shape (stats: bandit captain). Axel does not know that Ombrascura is hiding among his people and knows nothing about the scroll. If they ask for information, he sets a condition: to earn his trust they must bring him an Emerald, alive or dead. If they do, they will become Ruby and can wear the insignia. Until then, bandit-to-bandit respect.

**Searching for Ombrascura in the East:**

1. If they investigate (tavern, passersby) and ask about mages, they get no direct answers. A bandit (commoner) offers, for payment or if convinced/intimidated, a piece of information: "I saw mysterious sparks in the last house to the north of the eastern part one night, a week ago."
2. One of Ruby's guards knows a dragonborn who goes by Elemir Strunz, seen invoking the gods and making strange gestures.
3. Spells like Detect Thoughts or Telepathic Contact could allow gathering clues about Ombrascura, used on one of the Ruby members or on Ombrascura himself.

### West — The Emerald County

Between the east and west counties runs the main road leading to the castro. Guards from opposing factions control the passages. Going toward the castro is allowed for everyone; travelers without insignia can pass fairly easily if they don't arouse suspicion. Red-marked people are barred from going west and blue-marked from going east.

Frankie was an Emerald: he gets recognized and takes the PCs first to a comrade-in-arms, then to Miranda herself in the lord's palace in the northern part of the county.

**Emerald Assassins:** The PCs will be constantly watched and spied upon. The assassins emerge only at the opportune moment, attacking from the shadows with poisoned darts (sleep). They move in groups of 1d6. They do not fight to the death — they try to survive to bring information back to base. If captured and not searched immediately (DC 13), they kill themselves with a powerful poison. Captured ones give information only if tortured or if shown to be useful to Miranda.

**Miranda Emerald** tells the PCs:

- The same rule applies for her: to become Emerald they must bring a Ruby alive or dead, or one of them must defeat an Emerald champion in single combat without magic (stats: knight, p. 344)
- She knows Ombrascura and could recognize him. She knows the scroll can bring down the walls of Orcastle
- Like everyone, she knows the legend of the Torre di Torth but has no details

To obtain information from Miranda, the PCs will need to win her trust by demonstrating they have something of value or secret knowledge (information about Ombrascura or the scroll).

**Clues about the Torre di Torth:**

1. By investigating, the PCs discover the existence of a book that outlines, without details, the portal and the ritual. Miranda is willing to give it only if convinced with information or something else. Title: "Il mistero della torre di Torth"
2. Miranda's chamberlain, if questioned and convinced, knows the story with fewer details and the existence of the book

### Center — The Castro degli Highlander

The castro is neutral ground: everyone can enter in small groups. Fighting in the castro between factions is punishable by death. Only people with Ruby (red) or Emerald (blue) insignia can enter, if introduced by someone from the place. The entrance gate is heavily guarded.

In a stable on the right side of the castro were the griffons that Frankie had used to escape, but after his escape many tried to use them: all the griffons have fled or died.

**The Pergamena del Giudizio** is hidden north of the castro, under the flagpole. Ombrascura can retrieve it but is known as a Ruby, so if accompanied by Emeralds he could arouse suspicion.

If accompanied by Ombrascura, it will be easy to go retrieve the scroll under the flag to the north. Otherwise the PCs will need to:

1. Locate the place
2. Find the concealed compartment at the base of the flagpole — Perception/Investigation DC 14
3. Open the non-magical lock — HP 80
4. Avoid the magical trap triggered by a glyph

**Highlander Soldiers:** Well-equipped and disciplined, they rigidly control the central area. Organized patrols will try to capture the PCs if they are spotted.

**Lord Kreig Highlander** is arrogant and underestimates opponents who do not demonstrate a clear threat. He could be convinced to cooperate only if there is a personal reward. If questioned, the PCs discover he is a distant cousin of Malebranche (therefore able to activate the scroll if he knows the secret word, known to Ombrascura).

### North — The Torre di Torth and the Secret Well

> The tower rises menacingly and appears uninhabited. Black stone, a spiral of steep stairs leading to the room at the top. The air is thick with magical energy, and on the walls ancient inscriptions can be seen in a language long lost.

The Torre di Torth is an ancient temple dedicated to Vecna, the god of secrets and dark arts. It was built by the priest Ablunth Torth as a monument to dark power, with blood rituals and sacrifices. A History check at DC 12 reveals the connection between the tower and Vecna: the god of traitors and manipulators, who rewards those who scheme in the shadows.

The inscriptions on the walls can be deciphered only with Comprehend Languages or an Intelligence (Arcana) check at DC 15. They reveal: "Honor to Vecna, Vecna who opens the doors; I raise the chalice and toast to you."

**The room at the top of the tower:**

At the top, the PCs find two statues flanking a black stone altar. The statues depict figures wrapped in cloaks wielding scepters, with eyes set with onyx. In front of the altar is a stone basin full of ashes. The air is cold and vibrant with ancient power.

Above the altar, among dozens of other cups, hides the Coppa di Blackwood — needed to open the portal in the basement. If picked up and the inscription in Common on the cup is read ("Dark is the night in the hearts of the just"), Vecna appears.

**The Apparition of Vecna:**

Vecna appears as an avatar: he only wants to talk, cannot be hit and cannot strike. Amused by the PCs' efforts, he proposes a pact: he will remove the collar imprisonment if they complete a mission on his behalf (he gives no details). If they accept, he reveals how to use the cup as Dispel Magic. The PCs can also discover this function with Arcana DC 15. Detect Magic reveals only that it is abjuration. The magic word is "Praise Vecna" in Infernal: "Obloc Vecnal".

**The Ritual to Open the Well:**

An inscription reads: "Offer your blood, reveal a secret, and finally burn, my child, burn that which is already burned." A mural depicts the same clues: red drops, a fire, a shadow descending stairs emerging from an open well.

At least two of three steps are needed:

1. **Offer blood:** A PC offers their own blood on the altar (1d6 necrotic damage to themselves). The blood is absorbed by the stone and the basin emits a faint red glow.
2. **Evoke the past:** The group recounts aloud an important memory or a promise tied to the mission. Charisma (Persuasion) or Wisdom (Religion) check at DC 13. If they fail, the statues come to life and attack.
3. **Light the sacred flames:** A fire spell (Fireball, Burning Hands, or similar) must ignite the ashes in the basin. The gems on the statues glow and a loud noise is heard from the foundations.

If the ritual succeeds, the ground beneath the well opens revealing a stone staircase descending into the underground.

### The Underground Dungeon of the Tower

> You descend through the well into a narrow, dark corridor carved from raw rock. The air is cold and damp, the only sound is your breathing. The walls are covered with ancient runes that emit a faint blue light.

The dungeon consists of three trials protecting access to the portal:

**Trial 1 — The Hall of Reflection:**

A large hall with a floor polished like a mirror. As soon as the PCs set foot in the room, their doubles emerge from the floor and attack them. The doubles possess the same powers and HP as the original PCs, but are vulnerable to psychic attacks and cannot heal. Detect Magic reveals a powerful illusion spell; the damage dealt by the doubles is psychic.

*DM Note: manage the doubles as simplified copies of the PCs — they use only basic attacks (main weapon) and cantrips, not spell slots or class abilities. They have the same HP and AC as the PCs but have no active magic items. The goal of the hall is not a long combat but the discovery that ignoring the doubles is the solution.*

To pass the hall, the PCs must ignore the doubles and recite a phrase hidden in the runes on the opposite wall: "The shadow is nothing without the light, the shadow has the power you give it." Understanding this requires a Wisdom (Insight) or Intelligence (Arcana) check at DC 15. The shadows disappear after wounding at least three characters.

**Trial 2 — The Labyrinth of Vecna:**

A checkerboard floor appears before the PCs. On each tile there is a letter. They must spell out the word "V.E.C.N.A." by walking on the correct tiles. For each wrong letter, a poisoned dart deals 2d4+2 piercing damage to all characters (Dexterity saving throw DC 12 to halve).

**Trial 3 — The Guardian of the Portal:**

In the final hall, the PCs find the Teleportation Portal — an ancient circle of runic stones — guarded by a Stone Golem. The creature protects the portal from anyone who does not have the right to use it.

The PCs can fight the guardian or try to deceive it, convincing it they are the new custodians of the portal with Charisma (Deception) or Wisdom (Persuasion) at DC 18. A convincing roleplay performance grants advantage on the check.

**The Teleportation Portal:**

Once past the guardian, the portal requires the Coppa di Blackwood found at the top of the tower. A PC who succeeds on Arcana or History DC 15 understands the triggering rite. A fresco on a wall depicts the open portal and a cleric drinking from a cup. Toasting with the cup in honor of Vecna activates the portal. Any other god would not respond in a tower of this kind.

Once the toast is made, the tower trembles briefly and the PCs find themselves teleported near Malebranche's palace on the mainland.

## Key Clues

2. **The Pergamena del Giudizio** is hidden in the castro, in the underground beneath the flagpole to the north. Ombrascura put it there.
3. **The scroll** contains three pieces of information: a spell that brings down the invisible walls of Orcastle, a spell to send a message to Malebranche to prove they found it, and the word to send "Zikle l'Illuminato" (name of the creator, a good goblin wizard).
4. **Activating the scroll:** only a paladin, a character pure of heart, or Malebranche and his blood relatives can activate it. The secret phrase "The walls shall fall, the power shall end" must then be spoken, known to Ombrascura.
5. **Miranda Emerald** knows Ombrascura and could recognize him. She possesses the book "Il mistero della torre di Torth".
6. **Lord Kreig** is a distant cousin of Malebranche — information he has always kept hidden. He could therefore activate the scroll.
7. **The Torre di Torth** hides a teleportation portal in its underground, activatable with the Coppa di Blackwood and a toast to Vecna.
8. **The Coppa di Blackwood** can function as a Dispel Magic powerful enough to remove the collars. The magic word is "Obloc Vecnal" (Praise Vecna in Infernal).

## Finale

### The Scroll Dilemma

Once in possession of the scroll, the PCs must decide what to do with it. Malebranche has a meeting with his vassals in one week and intends to use the scroll to demonstrate that his power is intact and that he controls the only means capable of destroying the dome. The vassals are already plotting in secret: some suspect the dome is weaker than it appears.

**Three possible choices:**

- **Deliver the scroll to Malebranche:** The PCs go to the palace, Malebranche removes the collars and delivers the agreed 2500 gp. Malebranche strengthens his position, but the PCs remain indebted to an even more powerful tyrant.
- **Use the scroll to bring down the dome:** Malebranche is deposed, but chaos spreads throughout Orcastle. The PCs will have to face the consequences of an island at war.
- **Deliver the scroll to Ombrascura:** The collars are deactivated, but Ombrascura might betray them.

### Means of Escape

The PCs have four options to get out of Orcastle:

1. **Griffons:** No longer available — the last one was used by Frankie in his previous escape, the others died in a battle between Highlanders.
2. **Teleportation at the Torre di Torth:** The portal in the tower's underground, activatable with the Coppa di Blackwood and the toast to Vecna. Teleports near Malebranche's palace.
3. **Malebranche's flying ships:** Reachable from the door to the southeast on the outer peninsula or from above the castro. They normally don't land, so stealing one is complicated.
4. **Bring down the walls with the scroll and escape by ship:** Ships moored in the harbor near the outer peninsula, in poor condition but functional. Usable only if the magical walls fall.

### Malebranche's Palace

If the PCs manage to escape and decide to deliver the scroll, they must reach Malebranche's palace. There Malebranche removes the collar and delivers the agreed 2500 gp.

### Collars Deactivated or Expired

If the PCs free themselves from the collars or after the seventh rest, 3d6 mercenaries in Malebranche's pay are parachuted onto the outer peninsula in search of the scroll. Ombrascura has contacts at court who can cancel the glyphs, but when he does, Malebranche will decide to attack in force — an attack that demonstrates his weakness to the vassals and is politically dangerous for him.

- The Coppa di Blackwood (functions as Dispel Magic, word: "Obloc Vecnal")
- Alliances with the island's factions (Ruby, Emerald, Highlander) depending on choices made

## Items

### La Pergamena del Giudizio

The scroll's main power is to open the magical barrier blocking Orcastle. It contains three elements: the spell to bring down the walls, a message spell with the password "Zikle l'Illuminato" to send to Mesusu, and Malebranche's secret.

Activation: only a paladin, a character pure of heart, or Malebranche and his blood relatives can activate it. The secret phrase "The walls shall fall, the power shall end" must be spoken (known to Ombrascura). The scroll is protected by a powerful curse — Ombrascura knows the word but cannot use it, and is looking for an alternative way (his cleric Dispel Magic is not powerful enough).

### Coppa di Blackwood

Artifact found at the top of the Torre di Torth, among dozens of other cups on the altar. Functions as a Dispel Magic of sufficient level to remove the explosive collars. Activation word: "Obloc Vecnal" (Praise Vecna in Infernal). Detect Magic reveals only that it is abjuration. Arcana DC 15 to understand its function.

### Book "Il mistero della torre di Torth"

In Miranda Emerald's possession. Confirms the presence of a passage in the well beneath the tower, to be opened with a ritual performed at the top of the tower near the altar. Contains no other details. The legend, with fewer details, is known to everyone on the island.

### Explosive Collars

Control and coercion devices designed to eliminate the wearers in case of disobedience or failure. The glyphs are linked to the collars and placed in Malebranche's palace (modified version of Glyph of Warding).

**Explosion:** Radius 3m / 10ft / 2sq, 10d8 fire damage + 5d6 bludgeoning damage (average damage 45, maximum 110). Dexterity saving throw DC 16 to halve. On failure, the wearer is knocked prone and stunned for 1 round.

**Activation:** Attempted removal without the code, exceeding the time limit (seventh long rest), or remote activation by the jailer.

**Deactivation:** Intelligence (Arcana) or Dexterity (Thieves' Tools) DC 20. A natural 1 causes the collar to explode immediately (homebrew rule: in 5e a natural 1 is not an automatic failure on ability checks, but here it is for narrative tension). Dispel Magic of 5th level or higher works, or the deactivation command from whoever activated it.

**Ways to remove the collars:**

1. Dispel Magic — the PCs probably don't have the necessary power
2. The Coppa di Blackwood — is actually a Dispel Magic powerful enough
3. The pact with Vecna — the avatar offers removal in exchange for a future mission
4. Ombrascura's contacts at court can cancel the glyphs, but this triggers Malebranche's attack

## DM Notes

This is a sandbox adventure: the PCs can explore the four zones in any order. The collar timer (7 long rests) creates urgency but should not stifle exploration — the PCs have enough time to visit all zones if they don't waste days.

**Frankie Partenope** is the narrative guide. He starts talking only once on the island and knows crucial information: the two openings in the dome (above the castro, guarded; outer peninsula, opens every two weeks — too late), the legend of the Torre di Torth as a third escape route, and the fact that the griffons are no longer there. Frankie hates Malebranche because he was unjustly imprisoned.

**Ombrascura** is the true engine of the intrigue. He hides in the East as Elemir Strunz, a follower of Axel. His original plan — enter Orcastle with the scroll, free everyone, and end Malebranche's power — is not working because he cannot activate the scroll. He knows the legend of the tower and the existence of Miranda's book, but does not know if the contents are truthful.

**The factions** are in an unstable equilibrium. The PCs can ally with one or more factions, but every alliance has a price (bringing an enemy alive or dead, winning a duel). The insignia are important for moving between zones.

**The island map** has five key points: A1 (the castro, scroll in the underground), A2 (Torre di Torth, teleportation in the underground), A3 (main entrance to the castro, guarded), A4 (griffon stables, now empty), A5 (outer peninsula to the southeast, rare landing point for ships).
