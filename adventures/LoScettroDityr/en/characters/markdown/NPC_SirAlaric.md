## Description

Sir Alaric presents himself as an austere and devout man, with short-cropped gray hair and clear eyes that convey authority. He wears the ceremonial robes of the Keepers of the Balance with a naturalness that inspires trust. His voice is calm, measured — every word seems weighed and just.

Beneath the facade, Alaric is a man consumed by ambition. His eyes betray a greed that no prayer can hide, if you know where to look.

Distinctive trait: he has a habit of clasping his hands as if in prayer when he speaks — a gesture his followers find reassuring, but which is actually a nervous tic that betrays the calculation behind every word.

## Motivations

Alaric climbed the hierarchy of the Keepers of the Balance with patience and manipulation. His devotion is a mask: what he truly wants is absolute power. The Scepter of Tyr represents for him the means to transcend mortality and become semi-divine.

He is willing to sacrifice anyone — followers, allies, innocents — to achieve his goal. He doesn't consider himself evil: he sincerely believes the world needs a strong leader, and that he is the only one worthy of that role.

## DM Notes

- Alaric is an antagonist the PCs might initially mistake for an ally. His facade is convincing — don't reveal his true nature too soon.
- The Corruption Aura is a mechanical signal of his nature: PCs sensitive to magic or with high Perception can sense a "discomfort" near him.
- In combat he opens with Dominate Person on the most dangerous PC, then uses Fireball for area control. He uses Animate Dead as shields. He uses Invisibility to flee if below half HP.
- His past in the Ten Towns can be a hook for PCs with backgrounds tied to Icewind Dale.
