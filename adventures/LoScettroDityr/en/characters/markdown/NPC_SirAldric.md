## Motivations

Sir Aldric was placed to guard the Golden Brooch of Ostegard centuries ago. He doesn't remember his mortal life — only the duty. He judges anyone who approaches the Brooch: if the visitors demonstrate purity of intent, he lets them pass. If they lie or refuse the trial, he attacks without hesitation.

He doesn't desire to fight — he desires that someone finally be worthy.

## DM Notes

- Sir Aldric doesn't attack immediately. He poses the moral trial (three spirits of the PCs' victims) and judges the responses. He attacks only if the PCs lie (Deception DC 18 to deceive him) or refuse the trial.
- If the PCs answer sincerely and accept the consequences, the door opens without combat. This is the intended path.
- In combat he is a serious opponent: 2 attacks of 2d6+4+2d6 per turn, AC 18, 127 HP. For 5-6 PCs at level 10 it's a MEDIUM encounter.
- If defeated in combat, the Brooch is still accessible — but the PCs don't receive the guardian's blessing.
