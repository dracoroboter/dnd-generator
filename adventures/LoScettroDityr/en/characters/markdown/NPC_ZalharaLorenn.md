## Description

A middle-aged woman, anonymous and reassuring in appearance — exactly as she wants. Brown hair pulled back, round glasses, modest but clean scholar's clothing. She always carries a leather bag full of scrolls and books. She speaks in a calm, cultured tone, citing sacred texts of Tyr with ease.

Around her neck she wears a ring on a chain — the **Ring of the Evanescent Shadow**. If anyone asks, she says it's a family heirloom. The ring bears an inscription: *"turn left."*

Distinctive trait: she is so convincing in her role as a scholar that even Tyr's priests find her credible. Her best weapon is the trust she inspires.

## Motivations

Zalhara is a Vecna fanatic, devoted to the god of secrets with the same intensity she pretends to have for Tyr. Her mission is to spy on the PCs and report their every move to Othran Vorash.

She knows about the curse of the Scepter of Tyr — she knows what happens to whoever wields it without being worthy. This knowledge makes her dangerous: she could manipulate the PCs toward the Scepter knowing it will destroy them.

## Role in the Adventure

Zalhara presents herself to the PCs as a theology scholar interested in the history of Tyr and the Scepter. She offers information (true, mixed with strategic omissions) to earn their trust.

Her mission is to observe and report — not to fight. She avoids direct confrontation at all costs. If discovered, she uses the ring's charges to flee and reach Othran Vorash.

## DM Notes

- Zalhara is designed to be discovered *late*. The longer she stays in the group or near the PCs, the more information she passes to Vecna. The damage she does is informational, not physical.
- The documents in her bag are proof of her true identity — but the explosive trap (3d6 fire, Dex save DC 13) discourages the curious. The inscription on the ring (*"turn left"*) is the only clue to disarm it.
- If the PCs confront her with evidence, Zalhara calmly denies everything and tries to turn the accusations around. Only if cornered with no way out does she reveal her true nature — and at that point she tries to flee, not fight.
- **Clues for the PCs**: Zalhara knows too much about the Scepter's curse to be a simple scholar. An attentive PC (Insight DC 15) might notice that her knowledge of Vecna is suspiciously detailed for a devotee of Tyr.
