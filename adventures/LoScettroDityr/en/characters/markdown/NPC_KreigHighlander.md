## Description

A tall and imposing man, military bearing even when seated. Short-cropped gray hair, groomed beard, a thin scar crossing his left temple. He always wears polished plate armor — even when it's not needed. Dark blue cloak with the Highlander crest: a falcon on a mountain background.

He speaks with a booming voice, accustomed to giving orders and being obeyed. He looks down on his interlocutors — literally and figuratively. He has a habit of drumming his fingers on his sword hilt when bored.

Distinctive trait: arrogant and overconfident to a fault. He systematically underestimates his opponents — a flaw that could cost him dearly.

## Motivations

Kreig wants legitimate power. Controlling the Castro isn't enough — he wants to be recognized as lord of Orcastle. His secret kinship with Malebranche is both a resource (he knows the mechanisms of feudal power) and a burden (if discovered, his legitimacy would crumble).

As a former warlord, he believes order is imposed through force and discipline. He despises both Axel's crude brutality and Miranda's shadow maneuvers.

## Role in the Adventure

Kreig controls the Castro, the fortress at the center of Orcastle — the most important strategic position in the city.

**Modules A–C**: Kreig is one of the three faction leaders. The PCs can negotiate with him, but his arrogance makes negotiations difficult. He tends to treat the PCs as pawns, not allies.

**Module D**: Kreig becomes **Count Wildforge**, legitimate lord of the county. Miranda and Axel Ruby become his vassals. In this phase he actively cooperates with the PCs — he needs them to stabilize the territory.

## DM Notes

- The kinship with Malebranche is Kreig's most explosive secret. If the PCs discover it and make it public, Kreig loses legitimacy and Orcastle's political situation plunges into chaos.
- His arrogance is an intentional narrative flaw: the PCs can exploit it to manipulate him, and his enemies can use it to set traps for him.
- In Module D, Kreig is an imperfect ally: he cooperates with the PCs but always tries to maintain control. He doesn't take orders — at most "suggestions."
- In combat, Kreig is a competent but predictable Knight. He charges the front line, overexposes himself, and refuses to retreat even when it would be wise.
