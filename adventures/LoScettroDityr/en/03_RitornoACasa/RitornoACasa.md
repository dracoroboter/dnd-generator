### Variants Based on Module B's Ending

**If the PCs have the Scepter (options 2 or 4 of Module B):** the module proceeds normally. Tyr orders them to bring the Scepter to Svoalbard.

**If the PCs gave the Scepter to Alaric (option 1):** Tyr appears in a dream, furious. Alaric has been corrupted by the Scepter and is using its power to build an army. The PCs must first track down Alaric (who has retreated to the Ten Towns, his homeland) and recover the Scepter — by force or by cunning. Only then can they proceed toward Svoalbard. The DM must manage this additional quest before starting the journey.

**If the PCs destroyed the Scepter (option 3):** this module is not played. Tyr is satisfied — the destruction of the Scepter is one of the three good deeds. The PCs can proceed directly to Module D (Malebranche's curse triggers regardless of the Scepter). The DM must find an alternative hook to bring the PCs toward Waterdeep (e.g., Tungsten's wedding, a call for help from Kreig).

## The Scepter of Tyr

The Scepter of Tyr is a divine weapon that channels justice and purity, but is cursed for those who are unworthy. It can only be wielded by Tyr himself and by his herald, who must have proven their purity in the field. If the PCs investigate the scepter with *Detect Magic*, they find among other things the abjuration aura of Tyr's curse. An inscription on the handle in Elvish reads:

> "Only the pure are worthy to wield me, cursed by Tyr be the wicked."

The herald can pray to Tyr to reveal the details of the curse. That night Tyr appears in a dream:

> "I curse the wicked who dares touch the scepter. Justice shall rise like a burning blade in their dreams. Their life force shall fade day by day, every cure shall be in vain, until death or repentance claims them. Only a sincere act of redemption shall break the mark of my judgment. This is the curse of the Scepter of Tyr."

### Magical Properties

- **Sacred Resurrection** (one charge). Functions as the *Resurrection* spell. The Scepter judges the purity of the soul: a corrupted soul cannot be brought back to life. One time only.
- **Empowerment of Justice** (always, passive). All allies within 9m / 30ft / 6sq of the bearer receive: +2 to attack rolls, resistance to necrotic and psychic damage, advantage on saving throws against fear effects.
- **Shield of Purity** (bonus action, 1/day). Force field around the bearer and two allies within 9m / 30ft / 6sq. Reduces all damage taken by half for 3 turns.
- **Sentenza di Tyr** (1/day). The target creature must make a Charisma saving throw (DC 18). Failure: paralyzed for 1 minute, unable to lie or hide its true nature.

### The Curse of Tyr

The curse manifests whenever someone takes possession of the Scepter without the consent of the god of Justice.

- An invisible mark (perceptible by clerics of Tyr) seals the intruder's soul. Recurring nightmares, oppressive visions, reduced ability to rest and heal. Recovers only half HP after a long rest; potions and *Cure Wounds* do not work. The effect begins and lasts 24 hours even just from attempting to take the scepter, without succeeding.
- If one continues to possess the scepter, each day maximum HP are halved until the bearer dies.
- The effect ends only when the Scepter is returned **and** the offender performs a sincere act of repentance and redemption.
- Only the most powerful wizards (including Vecna) can avoid the effects. There is no other way.

### Zalhara Lorenn — Vecna's Spy

Zalhara claims to be a scholar seeking rare theological texts about Tyr in Luskan. In reality she has been ordered to spy on the characters and report to an emissary of Vecna upon arrival. She possesses a **Ring of the Evanescent Shadow** (refined jade ring, value 75 gp) containing 5 non-rechargeable charges of *Invisibility*. She has an illusion aura.

The spy aims to transmit to Vecna's follower every detail about the nature and protection of the Scepter: who guards it, what powers are known, the journey's stops, and how to avoid the curse. She will attempt to pass information to the local cell at each stop, unless discovered. She doesn't want to spend time with the PCs right after disembarking, precisely for this reason. The contacted local cell will attempt to steal the scepter.

Zalhara knows about the scepter's curse and will not attempt to steal it personally, especially not on the ship. She is looking for a way to avoid the curse, but doesn't know that there is no way other than being as powerful as Vecna himself.

If discovered and interrogated, she won't talk — she is too afraid of Vecna. She only says that it's a job for her and she was paid to watch them, without giving details. She may hint at the scepter's curse without going into specifics.

Around her neck she wears a small worthless ring with a strange spike. Inside it bears an inscription in Common: "*turn left*." It is the key to the trap on the documents.

**Searching Zalhara's cabin.** If the PCs search her cabin they find documents explaining her mission and how much she was paid (200 gp). The documents are protected by a mechanical explosive trap inside a bag attached under the bed. Investigation DC 12 to find it (automatic if they search the room for more than an hour or more than once at different times).

**Explosive Document Trap (Mechanical).** A small charge of black powder connected to a spring mechanism in the binder. If opened without the key or the correct procedure, the explosive triggers.

- Anyone within 1.5m / 5ft / 1sq takes 3d6 fire damage. Dexterity saving throw DC 13 to halve. The documents are reduced to ash.
- **Disarming:** Intelligence (Investigation) DC 15 to identify the trigger wire. Then Thieves' Tools DC 15 to disconnect the mechanism (DC 20 without thieves' tools).
- **Key:** The ring around Zalhara's neck. If turned in the wrong direction (the correct one is counterclockwise, "left") it explodes.

**Contents of the documents:** Zalhara was hired by **Othran Vorash**, a follower of Vecna who is also traveling toward Luskan. Othran departed before the PCs and has a head start of a few days — he is there waiting for information from the spy. Zalhara must gather information without being discovered and hire locals at each stop to attempt to steal the scepter. She is aware of the curse (knows it is deadly, doesn't know the details). She knows the name of the original herald (not the new one, if it was changed).

## Finale

> The altar of Tyr rises at the center of a sanctuary, illuminated by blue torches that flicker in the freezing air. On either side of the hall, statues of paladins with chipped swords seem to watch you in silence, like faceless judges. At the center, a great symbol of Tyr is carved into the floor and, before it, a shallow basin filled with clear water reflects everything like a mirror. Approaching, one notices a brief inscription urging the pilgrim to wash the lies from their heart and lay the Scepter upon the sacred stones.

The moment a character immerses their hands in the water to purify themselves, an echo of footsteps resounds in the room and illusory projections appear showing past injustices or hidden secrets. The group must confront these visions: for every deception or hidden guilt that is not confessed or repaired, the water in the basin darkens and the symbol of Tyr begins to crack. When the characters demonstrate they have faced their own faults, the illusion vanishes.

At that point the Scepter is placed on the altar; the blue flames intensify and a luminous figure — perhaps Tyr's emissary — appears to receive it. Only then, if every lie has been dispelled, a voice is heard speaking words of approval, and light fills the crypt: the Scepter is lifted in a golden beam, marking the end of the curse and the beginning of a new chapter.

The voice of Tyr echoes in the characters' minds:

> "Balance has been restored and my justice has prevailed over every lie. Yet the shadows of Vecna already stretch across the realms, lying in wait. Await a sign from me, o faithful of the light: when the hour comes, you shall raise your swords to annihilate the dark hunger of the God of Secrets. Place your courage in me, for the law shall never be broken."

### Othran Vorash's Plan

Othran is a faithful servant of Vecna, thirsting for forbidden knowledge and eager to seize the Scepter of Tyr to strengthen his own power. He sent Zalhara Lorenn as a spy to monitor the group, morally and physically weaken the characters by sabotaging the journey, and intervene at the right moment. Othran waits in secret at Luskan; as soon as Zalhara alerts him to a moment of vulnerability, he will strike with his acolytes.

If everything fails and the PCs reach Svoalbard, Othran attempts one last desperate move: appear at the sacred site and use his powers to seize the Scepter, trusting in an artifact or ritual that might mitigate the curse. His final gamble reveals how far he is willing to go for glory (or madness) in Vecna's service.

## Key Clues

1. The documents in Zalhara's cabin reveal Othran Vorash's name, his plan, and knowledge of the scepter's curse.
2. The letter of engagement signed "Z" found on the assassins in Neverwinter connects Zalhara to the attacks.
3. The bystander saved in Neverwinter knows the Dawnshield family and their address in Bryn Shander.
4. The book "L'altare di Tyr" (available in the libraries of Baldur's Gate, Waterdeep, or Neverwinter) mentions the Dawnshield family as keepers of information about the altar.
5. The note found on the cult fanatics confirms that Othran knows about the Dawnshield family.
6. The Lanterna dell'Aurora is the only way to find Svoalbard, which never stays in the same place.

## Milestone

No level advancement — the PCs remain at level 10. Returning the Scepter is the narrative reward of the module.

## DM Notes

This adventure is a long journey with many stops. Pacing is fundamental: alternate moments of tension (ambushes, pirates, dragon) with moments of respite (city stops, NPC interactions). Not every stop needs a combat — boredom and seasickness on the ship are full-fledged enemies.

**Zalhara** is the thread running through the first half of the journey. The DM must manage her carefully: she should not be discovered too early (it would trivialize the ambushes at Waterdeep and Neverwinter) nor too late (the players need the satisfaction of unmasking her). The documents in her cabin are the key — let the players find them if they actively search.

**Othran Vorash** appears three times: as a name in Zalhara's documents, in the encounter before Bryn Shander (where he flees immediately), and in the final ambush at Svoalbard. His presence should build gradually from abstract threat to concrete enemy.

**The Lanterna dell'Aurora** introduces an interesting mechanic for the journey to Svoalbard: it's not just a die roll, but a test of the group's moral consistency. The DM can use failures to create roleplay scenes where the PCs confront their own choices.

**The finale at the altar** is a pure roleplay scene. The illusory visions should recall real moments from the campaign — lies told, questionable choices, moral compromises. It is not a combat: it is a reckoning with oneself.
