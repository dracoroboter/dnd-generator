# Episode 2 — La Festa del Borgo

## Description

The Festa del Grazie is the event of the year in Fianus Romanus. Stalls, competitions, food, music, and the whole village in the square. The PCs arrive broke, hungry, and with a letter from "S" that promises something — or maybe not. Sergius is here, somewhere, and he's having fun.

## Objective

**Stated:** Find "S" at the Festa del Grazie, as the letter says.
**Actual:** Sergius doesn't let himself be found — he leaves another clue/taunt. The PCs meet new NPCs, get into trouble, and discover something about Sergius's clandestine trade.

## The Week Before the Festival

### 🎲 DM Notes

Quick montage — not a full session. Each PC picks an odd job from the table, makes a roll, gets paid. 10-15 minutes of play at most. The point is to establish that they're broke and the Festival is their only hope.

The PCs have **10 gp** (Gorim's advance). Rent is 4 gp/day. In a week they need 28 gp. They need to earn at least 18.

## New NPCs

### Flavia "La Biscia"

Traveling merchant, arrives from out of town for the Festival. Stat block: **Commoner** (MM p. 345). Sells fabrics, ribbons, and "exotic perfumes" (colored water). Talks extremely fast, changes subject every three seconds, impossible to follow. Knows everything about everyone because she travels between villages and collects gossip.

- **Trait:** talks without ever taking a breath, changes topic mid-sentence
- **Usefulness:** source of information about trade in the area — might know something about merchandise arriving by sea
- **Secret:** buys goods from a mysterious supplier who operates via the sewers (Sergius?)

### Mastro Cecio

Village blacksmith, enormous, silent. Stat block: **Commoner** (MM p. 345), STR 18. Speaks in sentences of three words maximum. Respected by everyone. Judges the arm wrestling contest.

- **Trait:** three words maximum per sentence ("Good." "Not bad." "Go away.")
- **Usefulness:** knows the sewers because he worked there years ago repairing grates. Knows there's "weird stuff" down there
- **Secret:** has seen someone entering the sewers at night, but doesn't care

### Donna Ortensia

Minor noble, wife of an official. Stat block: **Noble** (MM p. 348). Organizes the Festival (badly). Wants everything to be perfect, nothing is. Complains about everything in a shrill voice. The equivalent of Lopez: understands nothing but has decision-making power.

- **Trait:** "This won't do. Redo everything." (about anything)
- **Usefulness:** has access to the Count's castle (she's the only civilian who enters)
- **Secret:** she's a client of Sergius (buys embarrassing merchandise). Terrified it'll come out.

### La Contessina — Magnifica Vien dal Porto

The Count's wife. Stat block: **Noble** (MM p. 348). Arrives at the Festival in a carriage — *"La Contessina vien dal porto"* (The Countess comes from the port), everyone says with a mix of terror and resignation.

Born in **Vetusta Portus**, the seaside hamlet of Fianus. Her father was a dockworker at the port. She was a common woman whom the Count married after getting her pregnant. Now she puts on airs of a grand lady but never had the qualities for it. Prudish, bigoted, faux-cultured — she speaks in pseudo-refined words full of malapropisms. She's the one who had the "Gabella Pudicitiae" imposed after discovering the Count's portrait. Disliked by everyone, including Gorim.

- **Trait:** speaks in a pompous and incorrect manner. Every sentence contains at least one malapropism delivered with absolute confidence.
- **Usefulness:** she's the cause of the Gabella Pudicitiae. If the PCs understand her role, they understand why Sergius has it in for the Count.
- **Secret:** she's afraid the Count's portrait will surface — not for the Count's sake, but for her own. If it comes out that the Count bought "certain things," her reputation as a virtuous woman is finished.

**Typical phrases:**
- *"I am a woman of great moralitude, not like some others."*
- *"These things are an offension to public decency and to my personal sensibilitude. They give me the nerves."*
- *"The Count, my husband, is a man of mirrored rectitude — and whoever says otherwise is a defamator and a professional calumniator, and I'll sue them."*
- *"I come from a humble but honorific family. My father was a... a maritime entrepreneur."* (dockworker)
- *"I will not tolerate these... these... obscenitudes! Away! Away with all of it!"*
- *(to Gorim)* *"You should do more to protect the reputability of this family, instead of standing there eating."*
- *(to the PCs)* *"And who would you be? Adventurers? Shady people, surely. You can tell by the look of you."*

### Vecchia Tulia

The village old woman who knows everything about everyone. Stat block: **Commoner** (MM p. 345). The equivalent of Itala. Always sitting in the same corner of the square, apparently asleep, actually listening to everything.

- **Trait:** seems to be sleeping, then comments with surgical precision on something said 10 minutes earlier
- **Usefulness:** has known Sergius since he was a legitimate merchant. Knows about the old symbol. Could connect the double S to Sergius — but only if asked the right questions.
- **Secret:** none. Knows everything and has nothing to hide. This makes her terrifying.

## The Encounter with La Contessina

### 🎲 DM Notes

While the PCs wander the Festival with Gorim, a flashy carriage arrives in the square. Gorim sees it and curses under his breath: *"No. No no no. Not today."*

### 📖 Read to the players

> A golden carriage — or at least recently repainted gold, judging by the drips — stops in the center of the square. Out steps a woman in a dress too elaborate for a village festival and a hat that defies the laws of physics. She looks around with the air of someone who just stepped in something unpleasant.
>
> Gorim stiffens. *"La Contessina"*, he mutters through his teeth. *"Magnifica Vien dal Porto. Act natural, maybe she won't—"*
>
> *"GORIM!"*
>
> Too late.

### 🎲 DM Notes

La Contessina greets Gorim with fake cordiality. Gorim secretly hates her but must be respectful — she's the Count's wife.

While they talk, La Contessina notices something at the Festival that scandalizes her — a stall with decorative nymph statuettes (slightly undressed), or a painting of a bather, or simply a woman with a low neckline. Something absolutely harmless.

### 📖 Read to the players

> La Contessina freezes. She stares at the stall. Her face turns crimson.
>
> *"And WHAT is THIS?! Gorim! What is this stuff?! These... these obscenitudes in the square, in front of children, in front of EVERYONE?! Don't you know there's the Gabella Pudicitiae for exactly this?! I had it put in place! ME! To protect the moralitude of this village! And what do you do? NOTHING!"*
>
> She turns to the PCs.
>
> *"And who would you be? Adventurers? Shady people, surely. You can tell by the look of you."*
>
> Gorim, with the face of someone counting to ten: *"Contessina, these are... collaborators. Of the Count."*
>
> *"Collaborators? The Count didn't tell me anything. As usual. Anyway — get that stuff out of the square. NOW. Or I'll have everything shut down."*
>
> She storms off toward another stall to inspect.

### 🎲 DM Notes

The PCs now know that:
- There exists a **Gabella Pudicitiae** — a tax/ban on "indecent goods"
- La Contessina had it imposed
- Gorim detests her but can't say so
- La Contessina is the Count's wife (and therefore the indirect cause of Sergius's problems)

If the PCs ask Gorim about the Gabella Pudicitiae after La Contessina leaves:

> Gorim sighs: *"The Gabella Pudicitiae. The tax on... on stuff. That stuff. She wanted it. Ever since she found out that the Count... well, never mind. Point is, now if you sell anything that La Contessina considers 'indecent' — and she considers a bare elbow indecent — you pay triple taxes. And you have to declare who buys it. DECLARE. You understand? Nobody buys anything anymore."*

## Sergius's Clue — The Speech

### 🎲 DM Notes

**Grigius Voidus**, the grey official organizing the Festa del Grazie, is supposed to give the opening speech. Stat block: **Noble** (MM p. 348, CR 1/8). Sergius gagged him before the speech and took his place, in disguise.

The speech starts as a nonsensical ramble about quails and gratitude, then derails into allusions to Sergius's trade and veiled accusations against the Count.

### 📖 Read to the players — The Speech

> The official climbs onto the stage. The crowd falls silent. He clears his throat.
>
> *"Citizens of Fianus Romanus! On this solemn Festa del Grazie, it is my duty and privilege to remind you of the profound meaning of this celebration. The quails. Yes, the quails. Because without the quails, ladies and gentlemen, where would we be? The quails nourish us, the quails inspire us, the quails represent everything good this village has to offer. And we must be grateful. Grateful to the quails. Grateful to the harvest. Grateful to His Excellency the Count of..."*
>
> Pause. Smile.
>
> *"...whose name is on the tip of everyone's tongue, as always."*
>
> Chuckle in the crowd.
>
> *"But let's talk about serious matters. Let's talk about trade. About those fine products that arrive by sea and that certain gentlemen — I won't name names, but I salute them — purchase with such discretion. L'Assedio Notturno (The Night Siege), for instance. An excellent tonic, I'm told. Or perhaps you prefer La Rocca Imponente (The Imposing Fortress)? I hear it's very popular among officials. And what about La Bandiera al Vento (The Flag in the Wind) — the favorite of those who need... courage, shall we say, in the nighttime hours."*
>
> Silence in the square. The notables have gone white.
>
> *"And then there are the figurative arts. Beautiful portraits. Heroic. Some commissioned by very high-placed individuals — so high they never paid the bill. What a shame. The artist still has the work. Carefully preserved."*
>
> Long pause. Looks at the crowd.
>
> *"But I'm not here to cause a scandal. I'm here to play a game. The Count's Ring — yes, that one — is still in circulation. And it will go to whoever can follow the trail. The clues are already in place. Look where the Count never looks. Look where virtue is sold by weight. Look where the rats have more dignity than the nobles."*
>
> Theatrical bow.
>
> *"Happy Festa del Graz—"*
>
> He stops. A commotion from the blacksmith's shop. Someone shouts. The "official" looks toward the noise, smiles, and steps off the stage, slipping into the crowd. When someone tries to follow him, he's already gone.

### The Real Grigius Voidus

He's found in the blacksmith's storeroom, gagged but unharmed. He didn't see who attacked him. He only heard a voice saying: *"Nothing personal. Well, a little bit."*

Grigius is shocked and indignant. He doesn't understand what happened. He wants to file a complaint but doesn't know against whom.

## After the Speech — Terzilio

### 🎲 DM Notes

**Timeline:** At the start of the Festival, Sergius was in the square disguised as Terzilio (at the turnip stall). Then he changed to disguise himself as Grigius Voidus and give the speech. The real Terzilio arrives late to the Festival — he wasn't gagged, he's simply late (he's Terzilio, after all).

When the PCs meet him after the speech, it's the **real** Terzilio. He's never seen them — it was Sergius in Episode 1.

### 📖 Read to the players

> Among the stalls you find Terzilio, the greengrocer. He's setting up his stall in a hurry — he arrived late. Turnips and onions with a sign "FESTIVAL TURNIPS — SPECIAL PRICE" (triple the price).

### 🎲 DM Notes

If the PCs say something like "Terzilio, we saw each other last week" or "thanks for showing us the trapdoor":

> Terzilio looks at them puzzled: *"Me? You? Never seen you before. Want some turnips?"*

He's not lying — he really has never seen them. This is the first clue that the Terzilio from Episode 1 wasn't the real Terzilio. The PCs probably won't catch it right away, but the seed is planted.

### The Treasure Hunt

The clues in the speech send the PCs on a treasure hunt through the village. Three stops, prize at the end.

### 🎲 DM Notes

The PCs must decipher the three phrases from the speech. Each phrase indicates a location. At each location they find a note with the double S, a coin prize, and the clue for the next stop. If the PCs get stuck, Festival NPCs can help (Vecchia Tulia in particular).

---

**Stop 1 — "Where the Count never looks"**

**Solution:** The public notice board in the square — the Count never reads citizens' complaints. Investigation DC 10 or any village NPC confirms it.

**What they find:** Behind an old notice, an envelope with the double S. Inside a note: *"Well done. You're not entirely useless. Now look where virtue is sold by weight. — S."*

---

**Stop 2 — "Where virtue is sold by weight"**

**Solution:** The spice/herb stall at the market — it's where the "herbs" for vigor potions were sold before the ban. Investigation DC 12, or Flavia "La Biscia" knows, or Vecchia Tulia laughs and points to the stall.

**What they find:** Hidden in a sack of aromatic herbs, an envelope with the double S. Inside a note: *"Almost there. The last clue is where the rats have more dignity than the nobles. — S."*

---

**Stop 3 — "Where the rats have more dignity than the nobles"**

**Solution:** The **unionized rats'** stand in the square — the 5 rats with their accessories have a tiny stand with an illegible sign and a petition. They're the only ones in the square who openly say what they think, without hypocrisy. Investigation DC 13, or the PC with Speak with Animals asks the rats (who cooperate willingly — "Finally someone who takes us seriously!").

**What they find:** Under the rats' stand, a leather tube with the double S. Inside:
- An empty vial with a label: *"La Rocca Imponente — for the moments that count"* (logo: a halberd poking out of a bush — the brand of "L'Immane Alabarda")
- A scroll of parchment: a fragment of the **Count's portrait** — just a corner, enough to tell it's an "artistic" portrait but not enough to identify the subject
- A note: *"The rest of the portrait is safe. If your master wants it back, he knows where to find me. But I doubt he has the courage. — S."*
- A second note, in different handwriting, more hurried: *"I am not your adversary, good sirs. The day will come when you'll understand which side to be on. And that day, I assure you, it won't be your master's side."*
- A pouch with **3 gp**

### 🎲 DM Notes — Sergius Watches

While the PCs read the note, a PC with passive Perception ≥ 14 (or Perception DC 14 if someone looks around) notices a figure in the shadow of a nearby alley. Standing still, watching them. When they make eye contact, the figure gives a small theatrical bow — the same one from the speech on stage — and slips into the alley.

If the PCs pursue: the confrontation scene with Sergius (see § Sergius — if spotted). He whistles, 3 Thugs arrive, he vanishes.

If they don't pursue: the figure is already gone. But the PCs know that "S" was watching them. He's studying them.

## Sergius — If Spotted

### 🎲 DM Notes

No combat is planned for this episode. The only exception is if the PCs spot Sergius (in disguise). In that case:

**How they can spot him:**
- Notice that the "official" on stage has working hands (not a noble's) — Perception DC 16
- Follow someone who disappears into an alley after the speech — opposed Stealth
- At Stop 3, passive Perception ≥ 14 to notice the figure watching them

**If they confront him:**
Sergius has **1 turn** before vanishing. As he vanishes, he whistles — and **3 Thugs** (MM p. 350, CR 1/2) emerge from the crowd. They were disguised as porters/merchants. They cover Sergius's escape.

**Combat — 3 Thugs:**

**Encounter difficulty:** HARD (4 PCs lv2)

| Name | No. | HP | AC | Attack | Notes |
|------|----|----|----|---------| -----|
| Thug (MM p. 350) | 3 | 32 | 11 (leather armor) | +4 melee, 1d6+2 (mace) × 2 (Multiattack) | Pack Tactics |

- They block the PCs to prevent pursuit
- After **1 round**, or as soon as at least **2 are wounded**, the most wounded one flees
- The others fight until surrender or KO
- They don't fight to the death — if cornered they surrender ("They don't pay us enough for this!")

**Sergius (if hit before vanishing):**

| AC | HP | Speed | Notes |
|----|----|---------| -----|
| 14 | 18 | 30 ft. | Doesn't counterattack. Uses Ring of Shadows (Invisibility, bonus action in combat) and vanishes. |

**Sergius meanwhile:** uses the Ring of Shadows (Invisibility) and vanishes. The PCs hear footsteps receding, then nothing. It's not possible to capture him.

**If the PCs capture a Thug:** he knows little. "Some guy paid us 5 gp each to act tough if anyone followed him. We never saw his face. He contacted us with a note." The note has the double S.

**If the PCs don't spot Sergius:** no combat. The Festival ends peacefully.
