# Episode 1 — The Sewers of Fianus

## Description

The PCs are recruited by Sir Gorim Vel at the Taberna of Fianus Romanus to recover the Count's Ring from the village sewers. The Ring isn't there — they'll find a mocking letter that launches them toward Episode 2.

## Objective

**Stated:** Find the Count's Ring in the sewers and bring it back to Gorim.
**Actual:** The Ring isn't there. The PCs find a letter from "S" pointing to the Festa del Grazie.

> You arrived here for your own reasons — a delivery, a debt, a more or less voluntary exile — and found yourselves sitting at the **Taberna de Franciosibus aut Hispanardis**, the only tavern in Fianus Romanus.

- **Female:** "Misty", "Gowen", "Enlet", "Diamante", "Giustina" or "Ludovica"
- **Male:** "Ander", "Goffredo" or "Draco"

### 📖 Read to the players

None of the PCs have ever done any of this.

*DM Note: adapt names and feats to the PCs at the table. The example above is indicative — choose from the list based on character gender and invent absurd feats on the spot.*

### 🎲 DM Notes — PC Reaction

**If the PCs play along** (no roll required, just don't contradict him): Gorim is enthusiastic, treats them with deference, and the payment negotiation starts higher. Domina Lella eyes them suspiciously but stays quiet — if they're really famous heroes, maybe the rent will come.

**If the PCs get caught** (or give themselves away, or are honest): Gorim darkens. *"Ah. You're not... Ah."* Pause. *"Well, you'll do. Come with me. Count's orders."* The monetary offer drops further — it'll start even lower in the negotiation.

## The Scroll

### 🎲 DM Notes

The man is **Sir Gorim Vel**, the Count's official — whose name nobody can pronounce. He unrolls a scroll and reads:

### 📖 Read to the players

> *"Let it be known to whomever reads this proclamation that His Most Illustrious Excellency the Count of [ink stain], Lord of Fianus Romanus and faithful vassal of the Crown of Drakenhold, communicates with utmost regret and profound consternation that His most precious Ring — a relic of inestimable value and symbol of His august house — has been lost in circumstances as unfortunate as they were unforeseen, having slipped from His noble fingers and plummeted down the drain of His private latrine.*
> 
> *Therefore, the undersigned Sir Gorim Vel, envoy and trustee of His Excellency, is charged with recruiting a band of valorous adventurers, or at least available ones, so that they may descend into the village sewers and recover the aforementioned Ring, returning it intact and, if possible, clean.*
> 
> *The reward shall be commensurate with the value of the undertaking and the generosity of His Excellency, which is known to all, though no one has ever actually verified it.*
> 
> *Signed: [illegible signature]"*

*DM Note: the Count's signature should be "read" aloud as an incomprehensible pronunciation, different every time. Gorim tries seriously but the result is always a different and nonsensical sound ("Signed... the Count of Brrglmfh... no, Prùgnacchio... wait, Thlòmpferd..."). Nobody corrects him because nobody knows how it's actually pronounced. This gag should be repeated every time the Count's signature appears in any document.*

> Gorim rolls up the scroll, looks at you, and says: *"Good. Questions?"*

### 🎲 DM Notes — PC Questions

The PCs will have questions. Gorim answers everything with growing impatience:

- **"What is the Ring?"** — *"The Count's Ring. It's a ring. The Count's. What else do you need to know?"*
- **"What does it look like?"** — *"It's a ring. Gold. With a seal. About this big."* (indicates a different size every time)
- **"Why is it in the sewers?"** — *"Because His Excellency had a... hygienic incident. Don't ask questions."*
- **"Who is the Count?"** — *"The Count is the Count. Your employer. Enough."*
- **"What's the Count's name?"** — Gorim opens his mouth, stops, looks at the scroll, turns it over, and says: *"It's written right here. Clearly."* (it's not written clearly)
- **"How much are you paying us?"** — Opens the negotiation (see below).
- **Any other question** — *"Yes yes, details. Let's go, we're running late."*

When the questions run out (or Gorim loses patience), move to the negotiation.

## The Negotiation

### 🎲 DM Notes

The PCs, who owe **3 gold pieces** in rent to Domina Lella (who's standing right there listening with arms crossed), will inevitably ask: "And how much are you paying us?"

**If the PCs haven't been caught** (Gorim still believes they're famous heroes): starts at **30 gold pieces**. The PCs can negotiate up to 50.

**If the PCs have been caught** (or were honest): Gorim offers **1 gold piece**. One. The PCs owe 3 gp to Domina — the offer isn't even enough to pay the debt.

If the PCs accept the 1 gp offer without negotiating, Domina Lella grabs a rolling pin from behind the bar and threatens to call the guards: *"ONE coin?! And how am I supposed to see my three gold pieces?! Either you negotiate better or I'm calling the guards and having you thrown in!"* The PCs are forced to negotiate.

A bargaining scene opens. Gorim takes offense, the PCs insist, Gorim brings up that "in my day adventurers did it for the glory", the PCs respond that you can't eat glory, Gorim sighs, counteroffers 5 gp, the PCs push back again... The scene can go on as long as the table is having fun.

**Maximum result:** 50 gold pieces, but only for recovering the Ring. Gorim specifies: *"50 gold pieces. FIFTY. But only if you bring me the ring."*

**When he actually has to pay** (in later episodes), every payment is a comedic scene — see Gorim's NPC sheet (§ Pathological Stinginess).

Gorim will pay half the sum since the ring won't be found.

## The Search for the Entrance

The ring fell into the Count's latrine, so it must be searched for in the sewers. Gorim knows this and knows where the entrance is, but would prefer the PCs figure it out themselves — "If I have to do everything, what are you for?"

**Locations in the village** (see map `../maps/FianusRomanus`):

- The **Taberna de Franciosibus aut Hispanardis** is located at the **Mercato Centrale**, the village's main square.
- The **Count's residence** is the largest building in the **Aviano** quarter. Closed and guarded — the Count is never there, but the guards pretend he is.
- The **sewer entrance** is a trapdoor in the **Settebagni** quarter, past **Ponte Milvius**. The trapdoor is covered by Terzilio's cart.
- **Terzilio the greengrocer** sells turnips and onions. He has a stall at the **Mercato Centrale** but lives in **Settebagni** (where to find him is left to the DM).
- The **guards** are stationed at the Count's residence in **Aviano**.

### 🎲 DM Notes — The PCs Are Locals

The PCs are broke adventurers who've been living in Fianus Romanus for a while. They already know each other and know the village. The first scene at the Taberna serves to introduce the characters to each other for the players (not the PCs). There's no need to explain where Settebagni is or what Ponte Milvio is — they know.

### Domina Lella's Information

Domina Lella's information doesn't come all at once. It should be given at separate moments, in response to specific PC questions. Domina Lella wants at least a down payment on the rent (5 sp, or a drink offered, or a convincing promise — Persuasion DC 12) before she'll talk.

### 📖 Read to the players

> Domina Lella crosses her arms: *"Money first. Then information."*

### 🎲 DM Notes

After paying/convincing, Domina Lella answers questions one at a time:

**a) If they ask about the sewers:**
> *"The sewers? Should be down in Settebagni."*

**b) If they ask how to find them, or who can help:**
> *"Ask Terzilio, he's from there. Sells turnips and onions, you can find him here at the market too."*

**c) Casually, if the conversation continues:**
> *"That one's always complaining that someone keeps moving his cart."*

### 🎲 DM Notes

## Overview

The sewers of Fianus are surprisingly elaborate for a village of this size. Nobody knows who built them or why. They're a 5-room dungeon with a mix of constructed architecture and natural caverns.

**Why the Ring is here and not under the castle:** The sewers flow from east (Aviano, where the Count's castle is) toward west (Settebagni, where the entrance is). The current carried the Ring from the Count's latrine to these rooms, where Sergius found it.

**The tunnel:** From Room 1, a long tunnel heads east toward the castle. About 10 minutes of walking in dim light — dripping, echoes, rat noises, but nothing happens. The tunnel narrows progressively until it becomes impassable. The PCs can't reach the castle from here, but they understand that the sewers do connect Settebagni to Aviano.

**Map:** `maps/LeFogneDiFianus.png`

**Room connections:** Exploration isn't linear. From Room 1 the PCs can go to Room 2 (corridor above) or Room 3 (passage in the center). Room 4 is reachable from 3 and 5 (corridors on the right side of the map). Room 5 is accessible only through Room 4 (the door with the bureaucratic form). The PCs can explore in whatever order they prefer — the rooms work independently.

**Tone:** The problems are mundane but treated as epic. No real mortal danger — consequences are comedic (debts, embarrassments, ridiculous curses).

**Estimated duration:** 1-2 sessions (1-3 hours each).

## The Rooms

### Room 1 — The Flooded Entrance

Large circular hall with two connected areas (visible on the map). Stagnant water at ankle level, terrible smell.

**Encounter: The Unionized Rats**
A group of giant rats (5x **Giant Rat**, MM p. 327) physically blocks the passage to room 2. They're lined up, motionless, and won't move. Their leader, an unusually large rat with a tiny hat — **Biascica** — squeaks in what seems like an organized manner. It's a picket line.

Each rat wears an accessory: one has a scarf, one a glove, one a little hat (the leader), one socks, one a neckerchief. These are clearly stolen items.

The PCs must decide quickly: force their way through or find another way.

- **Combat:** The PCs attack. The rats fight but flee after 2 of them are defeated. The survivors will return in room 3 to cause trouble.
- **Brute force (Athletics DC 10):** Push them aside physically. Works, but the rats will follow them hostilely for the rest of the dungeon.
- **Food/payment:** If a PC offers a food ration or 5 copper pieces, the leader nods and the rats move aside in an orderly fashion.
- **Speak with Animals / Sylvan:** The rats have formed a union and are demanding a "toll." If paid, they offer advice: "Don't trust the one in room 2." If a PC has Speak with Animals (or similar ability), the conversation can be much richer — the rats have opinions about everything and everyone in the sewers.

**DM Note:** The 5 unionized rats are recurring NPCs. They should be reused in later episodes (protest in the square at the Festa del Grazie, picket line in front of a shop, etc.). They're recognizable by their accessories.

**Loot:** Among the debris, an oxidized copper ring. It's not the Count's Ring. Gorim (if present) examines it and says "No. Next room."

### Room 2 — The Sewer Sage

Natural cavern with stalactites and a raised dry area. Someone has placed a chair, a small table, and a candle there.

**NPC: Ermolao "Il Profondo"**
A middle-aged man who lives in the sewers by choice. Former village accountant, he quit and moved down here "to find himself." Speaks cryptically but actually says obvious things.

- "The Ring you seek... is not where you think it is." (he knows nothing)
- "The Count... ah, the Count..." *dramatic pause* "...is a very important person." (end)
- If they ask the Count's name: "I knew it, I knew it... wait..." *falls asleep*

**Insight DC 10:** Ermolao knows absolutely nothing useful but genuinely believes he does.

**Usefulness:** If treated well, he draws a "map" of the sewers on a napkin. It's wrong, but correctly indicates that in room 5 there's "something important."

---

### Room 3 — The Great Cavern (The Tax Collector Troll)

The largest central cavern on the map. Open space, natural columns, echo.

**Encounter: Grunzio the Sewer Troll**
He's not a real troll (that would be too much for level 1 PCs). Use the stat block of an **Ogre** (MM p. 237) with the following modifications:

- HP reduced to 30 (instead of 59)
- Speaks Common (poorly)
- Not hostile by default

Grunzio has lived here for years and considers the sewers his home. He just wants someone to pay his back rent — 47 gold pieces and 3 copper, "exact exact." He doesn't know who to pay the rent to. He doesn't know why he has to pay it. But he's convinced he must.

**If the PCs insist on violence — 3-phase escalation:**

1. **First shove:** Grunzio pushes the nearest PC (no damage, the PC ends up on the ground in the filthy water). *"NO! Grunzio doesn't want! Talk! TALK!"*
2. **Second shove:** Grunzio grabs a PC and throws them against the wall (1d4 bludgeoning damage). *"ENOUGH! Grunzio not bad! YOU bad!"*
3. **Third time:** Grunzio strikes for real with his club — full Ogre damage (2d8+4). The PC probably goes down. Grunzio immediately stops, starts crying, kneels beside the wounded PC and clumsily tries to help. *"No no no no... Grunzio didn't mean to... Grunzio bad... forgive Grunzio..."* He tries to bandage them with a piece of his filthy tunic. Combat ends here — Grunzio won't attack again.

**Peaceful options:**

- **Persuasion DC 12:** Convince him the PCs are the tax collectors and the rent has been forgiven. Grunzio cries with joy and lets them pass.
- **Deception DC 10:** Tell him the rent is paid in room 5 and he should go there. Grunzio leaves the room.
- **Performance DC 13 (Pipinius?):** Singing him a song moves him and makes him friendly. He offers the PCs a "treasure": a bucket full of bottle caps.

### Room 4 — The Bureaucratic Trap

Hexagonal constructed room, more polished than the others. A closed door with a mechanism.

**Trap: The Form**
The door can't be opened by force or lockpicking. Next to it is a lectern with a form to fill out: "REQUEST FOR ACCESS TO THE NEXT ROOM — Form 7B/bis". It asks for:

- Full name of the applicant
- Reason for visit (max 3 words)
- Name of the Count (required field)
- 

The "Name of the Count" field accepts any answer. The door opens regardless. But a flat, bureaucratic magical voice says: *"Missing the stamp."* Nothing else happens. The door is open. If the PCs go back to look for a stamp, they waste time — no stamp exists. If they ignore it and proceed, everything's fine. Purely psychological trap.

The form is magically filed in a basket near the door.

**Investigation DC 12:** The form has been filled out hundreds of times. Each entry has a different name for the Count. No two are the same.

### Room 5 — The "Treasure" Room

Constructed room, relatively clean — too clean compared to the rest of the sewers. In the center, a pedestal with a chest. But that's not all: along the walls there are **open wooden crates**, some empty, others with packing straw residue. The floor is marked with **recent boot prints** — someone comes here regularly. These aren't monster prints: they're men's boots, of good quality.

**Investigation DC 12:** The crates have a brand burned into the side — a double interlocking S (like a dollar sign), the same seal as the chest.

**Investigation DC 15:** The crates contain only packing straw. But from the shape of the impressions in the straw and the size of the crates, the PC can intuit the general type of merchandise contained — medium-sized objects, probably individually wrapped. (The DM decides what they are when defining Sergius's trade.)

This room is **Sergius Arimano's** warehouse — the place where he collects his merchandise before selling it. The PCs don't know this yet, but the double S will return.

**The Chest**
Open, no trap. The seal isn't the Count's — it's an unknown seal, a double interlocking S (the mark of Sergius Arimano, which the PCs won't recognize yet). Inside:

- A letter
- A pouch with 15 silver pieces and a note: *"For you ragamuffins."*
- A dried onion ring (not the Count's Ring)

**The Letter:**
*"Were you looking for something? Too bad. I got here first. Your master's ring is safe — cleaner than when I found it, I assure you. If he wants it back, he'll have to do much better than sending rats into the sewers to look for it. At the Festa del Grazie, perhaps, there'll be something for you. Or perhaps not. Depends on how much you amuse me. — S."*

The letter is the hook for Episode 2 and the first clue to the existence of Sergius Arimano.

## Episode Closing — Return to the Taberna

### 🎲 DM Notes

The PCs climb out of the sewers and show the letter to Gorim. He reads it, turns red in the face, rereads it, turns it over as if there might be something on the back.

### 📖 Read to the players

> Gorim stares at the letter in silence for an embarrassingly long time. Then he folds it carefully, tucks it into his belt, and looks at you.
> 
> *"Good. You didn't find the ring. You found a problem. Which is worse."*
> 
> He pulls out a coin purse, weighs it in his hand, sighs deeply, and counts out half the agreed sum — one coin at a time, with the face of someone donating a kidney.
> 
> *"Here. Half. You earn the rest by continuing to work for me. This 'S' — whoever they are — needs to be found. And the ring needs to be recovered. At the Festa del Grazie, the letter says? Good. We'll be there."*
> 
> Pause.
> 
> *"And this time try not to come back empty-handed."*

### 🎲 DM Notes

The PCs receive half the sum agreed in the negotiation. If they had agreed on 50 gp, they receive 25. If they had agreed on 30, they receive 15. And so on. Gorim counts every coin with visible pain.

This binds the PCs to Gorim for the following episodes — they must continue working for him to get the rest. The hook for Episode 2 (the Festa del Grazie) is established.
