# The Count's Ring

## Introduction

The realm of Drakenhold is enjoying its most radiant season. In the south of the realm, the village of **Fianus Romanus** — a sun-kissed hamlet surrounded by fertile lands — is the kind of place where nothing important ever happens. At least until **Sir Gorim Vel**, the Count's official, decides it's time to recruit a group of adventurers for a mission "of capital importance": finding **l'Anello del Conte** (the Count's Ring).

Nobody knows what the Ring is. Nobody knows what it does. Nobody knows where it is. And nobody knows the Count's name.

Welcome to Fianus Romanus.

---

## General Plot

The PCs are hired by Sir Gorim Vel to recover the Count's Ring, which fell into the sewers of Fianus Romanus. They descend into the sewers and find only a mocking letter: someone — signed "S" — took the Ring before them and promises to show up at the Festa del Grazie. Gorim pays half the fee and binds them to him for the follow-up.

## Concept

A D&D 5e (2014) episodic adventure, inspired by the Italian comedy series **Boris**. Humorous tone without going overboard: absurd situations treated seriously by the characters. Bureaucracy, incompetence, oversized egos. Structured as "episodes" like a TV season.

The adventure is the **prologue** to the serious campaign **Il Re Spezzato** (The Broken King). The Count is a local noble, not King Dorian Valemarch. In the final episode, just as the PCs are about to meet him and earn honor and glory, the Count dies in an explosion. No answers, no reward, no name. The Count's death and the ensuing chaos are the hook that drags the PCs into the larger story of Drakenhold and Il Re Spezzato.

## Lore

**Drakenhold** — The name refers to both the **realm** and its **capital**, at the center of the continent. Ruled by **King Dorian Valemarch**.

**Fianus Romanus** — A village in southern Drakenhold, connected to the capital. Surrounded by fertile lands, it faces the sea to the south (like Ostia to Rome). Governed by the Count — a vassal of Dorian whose name nobody knows. Rural life, trade, traditions. Everyone knows everyone, everyone gossips, nobody works as much as they should. It has surprisingly elaborate sewers for a village of this size (nobody knows why). The population is almost entirely human — other races are rare and attract curiosity.

## Running Gag

### The Count's Name

Every time an NPC is about to say the Count's name, they get interrupted:

- A deafening noise that covers the word
- A sneeze
- Something collapsing
- "The Count... what's his name... wait, it's on the tip of my tongue..."
- Official letters have the seal but the signature is illegible
- An NPC swears they know it but wants to be paid first — then doesn't know it either

### The Ring

Everyone talks about it, nobody knows what it does, nobody knows where it is:

- Every NPC has a different and incompatible theory
- Someone swears they've seen it (it was an onion ring)
- Gorim is convinced the PCs already know everything
- It's only found in the final episode, where "everything will be clear to everyone"

### Gorim's Arc (SPOILER — DM only)

After the explosion that kills the Count, Gorim changes. He's no longer the exasperated official — he's a man who lost his lord and couldn't do anything about it. Between L'Anello del Conte and Il Re Spezzato, **one year** passes. In that year Gorim is summoned to court and becomes King Dorian Valemarch's confidant and servant. When the PCs find him again in Il Re Spezzato, he's a different man: serious, weary, loyal to the bone. The contrast with the comedic Gorim from L'Anello del Conte is the strongest signal that the tone has changed.

During the comedic episodes, seed small moments where Gorim shows a loyalty and sense of duty that go beyond the gag — these are the traits that Dorian will notice and that will lead him to choose Gorim as his confidant.
